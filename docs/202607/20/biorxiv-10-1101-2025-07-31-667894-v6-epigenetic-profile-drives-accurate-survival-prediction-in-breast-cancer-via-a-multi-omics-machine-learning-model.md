## 摘要
准确的总体生存预测是个性化治疗乳腺癌的关键，但仅靠突变负荷是不够的。为提高预后准确性，我们整合了802名乳腺癌患者的基因组、转录组、蛋白质组、表观遗传和临床特征，开发了随机生存森林模型BANDOL（Breast cancer Analysis with Neoplastic Data and Omics Learning）。BANDOL正确预测了75%患者对的生存排序，并优于基于突变的模型（时间依赖性AUC：0.9-1 vs. 0.4-0.9）。免疫激活特征与治疗后更长的总体生存相关，而更短的总体生存与TREM2髓系细胞、B细胞和瘦素信号相关。模型稳健性在三个独立的癌症基因组图谱队列（子宫癌、卵巢癌和胶质瘤）中得到验证。表观遗传特征对BANDOL的总体生存预测能力最强。当前疗法可与靶向ME3、PPARG、OLIG3和SLC25A22基因甲基化的策略相结合。本研究证明了通过机器学习进行多组学整合可增强生存预测，并揭示可操作的生物标志物。

## Abstract
Accurate overall survival (OS) prediction is key for personalized treatment in breast cancer, but mutation burden alone is insufficient. To improve prognostic accuracy, we integrated genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, epigenetic, and clinical features from 802 breast cancer patients to develop BANDOL (Breast cancer Analysis with Neoplastic Data and Omics Learning), a Random Survival Forest model. BANDOL correctly predicted survival ranking in 75% of patient pairs and outperformed mutation-based models (time-dependent AUC: 0.9-1 vs. 0.4-0.9). Immune activation signatures correlated with a longer OS after therapy, while a shorter OS was linked to TREM2 myeloid cells, B cells, and leptin signaling. Model robustness was confirmed in three independent The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) cohorts (uterine, ovarian and glioma cancers). Epigenetic features were the strongest OS predictors for BANDOL. Current therapies may be combined with strategies to target the methylations of ME3, PPARG, OLIG3 and SLC25A22 genes. This study demonstrates that multi-omics integration via machine learning enhances survival prediction and reveals actionable biomarkers.
