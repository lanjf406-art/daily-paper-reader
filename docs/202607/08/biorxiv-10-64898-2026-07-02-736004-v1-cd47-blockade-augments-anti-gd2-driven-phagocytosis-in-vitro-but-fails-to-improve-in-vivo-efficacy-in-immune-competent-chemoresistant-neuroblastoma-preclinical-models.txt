30 Introduction 

31 Neuroblastoma (NB) is the most common extracranial solid tumour of infancy and 32 early childhood, and outcomes for high-risk disease remain poor despite intensive 33 multimodality therapy. Immunotherapy aims to engage host immunity to recognise and 34 eradicate tumour cells. Anti-GD2 monoclonal antibodies, directed against the widely 35 and highly expressed NB antigen disialoganglioside GD2, are now clinically 36 implemented. When paired with chemotherapy such as temozolomide/irinotecan or 37 cyclophosphamide/topotecan, this chemoimmunotherapy approach improves 38 progression-free survival at relapse versus historical cohorts and has become the 39 platform for rational combinations in ongoing trials(1–6). 40 CD47 is an ubiquitously expressed transmembrane receptor of the immunoglobulin 41 superfamily(7,8). Its principal ligand, Signal Regulatory Protein alpha ( SIRPα) , is found 42 on myeloid cells (monocytes, granulocytes, dendritic cells and haematopoietic stem 43 cells) and delivers an inhibitory “self” signal(9). This function was first established on 44 erythrocytes, for which loss of CD47 marks senescent red cells for clearance by red-45 pulp macrophages(10). Across many human cancers CD47 is overexpressed, 46 prompting development of CD47-SIRPα blocking agents (antibodies and SIRPα-

47 based binders); comprehensive reviews summarise the preclinical and clinical 48 experience(11–14). Given the natural expression of CD47 on haemopoietic cells, Fc-49 competent anti-CD47 agents may also effect haematological toxicity through off-50 tumour opsonisation. ALX301 was therefore designed as a Fc-silent CD47 blocker, 51 comprising a high affinity SIRPα D1 domain (engineered for increased binding to 52 mouse CD47) fused to mouse IgG1 Fc with point mutation N297A to abrogate Fcγ 

53 receptor engagement while preserving CD47 binding (Fig. 1a).

54 Macrophages clear antibody-opsonised targets through Fcγ receptor-mediated 55 antibody-dependent cellular phagocytosis (ADCP) , if pro-phagocytic “Eat-me” cues, 56 (e.g. calreticulin), outweigh inhibitory “Don’t Eat Me” signals including CD47. Blocking 57 CD47 can tip this balance toward phagocytosis(15). At the tumour-macrophage 58 synapse, anti-GD2 provides the opsonising Fc to engage Fcγ receptors. Anti-GD2 also 59 blocks GD2 binding to its inhibitory ligand, Siglec-7, and can increase calreticulin 60 expression. CD47 blockade simultaneously removes the inhibitory brake (Fig. 1b). 61 Together, these mechanisms enhance macrophage-mediated tumour clearance, as 62 shown in prior NB studies(16). 63 Here we validate immune competent, chemotherapy-resistant genetically engineered 64 NB models that recapitulate key features of the human NB tumour 65 microenvironment(17). We use these models to test whether ALX301 augments anti-66 GD2, and whether any added benefit persists when layered onto a clinically aligned 67 temozolomide/irinotecan chemotherapy backbone. We show that CD47 inhibition 68 increases anti-GD2 driven phagocytosis in vitro but these effects are not translated in 

69 vivo , failing to improve anti-GD2 monotherapy or chemoimmunotherapy efficacy in two 70 independent models. Both models exhibit a myeloid-rich, suppressive 71 microenvironment, suggesting that the myeloid niche, not target presence, limits 72 benefit and that macrophage-reprogramming strategies may be required to unlock 73 value from CD47-axis therapy in NB. 74 Methods

220 Establishment and immune contexture of chemoresistant, immune competent 

221 NB models. NB is an embryonal tumour derived from neural crest progenitor cells. 222 MYCN gene amplification and ALK activating mutations are associated in promoting 223 aggressive tumours in ultra-high-risk NB patients(24,25). To model these tumours in 

224 vivo , genetically engineered mouse models (GEMMs) have been generated to 225 overexpress MycN and ALK F1174L downstream of the rat tyrosine hydroxylase ( Th )226 promoter(20,26). Genes downstream of the Th promoter are expressed in committed 227 sympathetic precursor cells of the neural crest. Expression of oncogenes Th -MycN 

228 and Th-ALK F1174L give rise to tumours in the sympathetic ganglia and adrenal gland, 229 locations commonly seen in human NB. 301 We provide a translational stress test of CD47 blockade in combination with anti-GD2 302 immunotherapy or chemoimmunotherapy in immune competent, chemoresistant NB 303 mouse models. A key contribution of this study is the introduction and immune 304 mapping of two immunocompetent, chemoresistant neuroblastoma models, WIN6 305 (C57BL/6) and TAM6 (129SvJ). Both models express the clinical target GD2 and the 306 inhibitory ligand CD47, engraft reproducibly, and recapitulate the myeloid-rich, 307 immunosuppressive niche seen in human neuroblastoma, including abundant MDSCs 308 and M2-like macrophages (with TAM6 particularly myeloid-dominant). These features 309 make WIN6 and TAM6 useful beyond the CD47 question: they enable clinically aligned 310 dosing studies and testing of new combinations, and their intact TME allows 311 interrogation of cell-cell interactions by flow, spatial, and single-cell readouts. The 312 observation that CD47 blockade did not enhance anti-GD2 antibody or 313 chemoimmunotherapy in these stringent models is informative. Together, WIN6 and

In such niches, removing the 329 CD47 “Don’t Eat Me” signal alone may be necessary but not sufficient to trigger 330 productive macrophage-mediated tumour clearance. Our data suggest the field would benefit from 347 investigating rational myeloid reprogramming to unlock CD47 blockade capabilities. 348 There are several therapies already validated for preconditioning myeloid cells in the 349 context of antitumour therapies, included are CSF1R blockade (to reduce suppressive 350 macrophages)(29,30), CD40 agonists or STING/TLR agonists (to license APCs and 351 skew macrophages)(31–33) or low-dose radiation (to boost “Eat Me” cues)(34). Next steps should include on-treatment 357 TME sampling and tests of myeloid-conditioning combinations with optimised 358 scheduling; if tolerated, increased ALX301 exposure could also be explored. These 359 efforts will clarify if, and under what conditions, CD47 blockade adds value in 360 neuroblastoma.
