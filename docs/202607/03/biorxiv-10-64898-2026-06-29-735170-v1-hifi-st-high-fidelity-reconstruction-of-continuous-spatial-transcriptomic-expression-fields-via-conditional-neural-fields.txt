14 Abstract 

15 Spatial transcriptomics characterizes tissue-scale gene expression patterns, yet its 

16 observations are sparse discrete samples of an underlying continuous molecular 

17 field, leading to spatial aliasing and sub-resolution information loss. Existing methods 

18 usually formulate this task as spot-level point regression, making it difficult to capture 

19 both expression continuity and the regional nature of observation. Here, we propose 

20 HiFi-ST, a conditional neural field framework for continuous spatial transcriptomics 

21 modeling. HiFi-ST formulates spatial gene expression prediction as continuous

23 support domain, approximates local integration through Monte Carlo sampling, and 

24 integrates multiscale tissue feature extraction with FiLM-based conditional 

25 modulation to improve modeling of complex spatial heterogeneity and consistency 

26 with the underlying measurement process. Systematic evaluation on three 

27 independent datasets (HER2+, cSCC, and Alex_NatGen) showed that HiFi-ST

29 metrics. On HER2+, HiFi-ST achieved an average PCC improvement of 65.1% and 

30 an average MSE reduction of 40.9%; on cSCC, PCC improved by 10.2% and MSE 

31 decreased by 51.2%; on Alex_NatGen, PCC improved by 80.0% and MSE 

32 decreased by 16.3%. In addition, the learned multiscale tissue representations 

33 supported downstream spatial immunoanalysis, including assisted identification of 

34 candidate TLS regions. Overall, HiFi-ST provides a unified framework bridging 

35 discrete measurements and continuous expression field reconstruction for tumor 

36 microenvironment analysis and spatial immune structure characterization.

39 40 Introduction 

41 Gene expression in biological tissues is intrinsically continuous, whereas current 

42 spatial transcriptomics technologies discretely sample the underlying molecular field

43 because of physical capture-array spacing, causing spatial aliasing and sub-

44 resolution information loss [1-3] . Predicting spatial gene expression from H&E images 

45 has therefore become an important strategy for mitigating these limitations [4-6] .

46 Representative methods include Vision Transformer-based sequence modeling, 

47 graph neural network-based topological modeling, and multimodal contrastive 

48 learning approaches such as HisToGene [7] , His2ST [8] , BLEEP [9] , and mclSTExp [10] .

49 However, these methods still follow a discrete spot-level supervision paradigm and 

50 do not explicitly model expression continuity or regional observation [11, 12] .

51 To address this issue, we propose HiFi-ST, a conditional neural field [13] framework 

52 for continuous spatial transcriptomics modeling. HiFi-ST reformulates spatial gene 

53 expression prediction as continuous expression field learning, models each spot as a 

54 regional observation over a finite support domain [14] , and approximates local 

55 integration through Monte Carlo sampling. It further combines multiscale tissue 

56 feature extraction with FiLM-based conditional modulation [15] to improve modeling of 

57 complex spatial heterogeneity. 

58 Our contributions are threefold: (i) reformulating spatial gene expression prediction 

59 from discrete point-wise regression to continuous expression field learning; (ii) 

60 explicitly introducing regional observation modeling to better match the measurement 

61 mechanism; and (iii) integrating multiscale conditional modulation with conditional 

62 neural field decoding for high-fidelity reconstruction of continuous expression 

63 patterns in complex tissues.

65 HiFi-ST Framework 

66 Each tissue section is associated with an H&E image 𝐼 and a set of sequencing 

67 locations 𝑥 𝑖 with known spatial positions. HiFi-ST takes multiscale histological 

68 images and spatial coordinates as input, reconstructs gene expression in continuous 

69 space, and obtains spot-level predictions through regional sampling and 

70 aggregation. The framework consists of four steps: data preprocessing, multiscale 

71 feature extraction, conditional modulation, and random-sampling-based prediction 

72 and aggregation.

178 Model performance was evaluated from three perspectives: numerical accuracy of

241 Baseline models 

242 In this study, five representative baseline methods were selected: 

243  HisToGene [7] : This method uses a Vision Transformer to perform sequence 

244 modeling on histological images, captures long-range dependencies through the 

245 self-attention mechanism, and regresses gene expression. 

246  His2ST [8] : This method explicitly models spatial adjacency relationships among 

247 spots and combines visual feature extraction with graph convolution for local 

248 structural modeling. 

249  THItoGene [18] : This method integrates dynamic convolution and capsule 

250 networks to enhance the adaptability of feature representations and the ability to 

251 model hierarchical relationships. 

252  BLEEP [9] : This method constructs a shared embedding space between images 

253 and gene expression profiles through contrastive learning, enabling zero-shot 

254 expression retrieval based on image queries. 

255  mclSTExp [10] : This method jointly models visual, spatial, and expression 

256 information as a multimodal sequence and achieves modality alignment through 

257 contrastive learning.

261 Alex_NatGen and 2e-5 for cSCC. Models were trained for 100 epochs with batch

265 Benchmark datasets and cross-validation strategies 

266 We evaluated HiFi-ST on three public spatial transcriptomics datasets: HER2+ [21] ,

267 cSCC [22] , and Alex_NatGen [23] (Supplementary Table S1). HER2+ and cSCC were 

268 generated using Legacy ST and were used to evaluate reconstruction under sparse 

269 supervision and strong structural heterogeneity, respectively. Alex_NatGen was 

270 generated using 10x Visium and was used to assess generalization under dense 

271 sampling and cross-patient distribution shift. 

272 To reduce bias from spatial autocorrelation, all experiments adopted slice-level 

273 partitioning, such that all spots from the same section belonged exclusively to either 

274 training or test data. Five-fold cross-validation was used for HER2+ and cSCC, 

275 whereas leave-one-out cross-validation was used for Alex_NatGen. Spots outside 

276 tissue regions or annotated as artifacts were removed during preprocessing.

383 Ablation experiments and core component results 

384 Systematic ablation experiments were conducted on HER2+, cSCC, and 

385 Alex_NatGen to examine the contributions of spatial encoding, multiscale modeling, 

386 conditional modulation, regional sampling and aggregation, HEG optimization, and 

387 model capacity. Overall, HiFi-ST gains arise from the synergy of high-frequency

389 modeling rather than from any single module. Detailed results are provided in 

390 Supplementary Tables S6–S13 and Supplementary Figure S5. 

391 Discussion 

392 The discrete sampling mechanism of spatial transcriptomics limits the 

393 characterization of continuous expression fields. To address this issue, we propose 

394 HiFi-ST, a continuous spatial expression reconstruction framework based on 

395 conditional neural fields. Unlike conventional spot-level point regression methods, 

396 HiFi-ST integrates continuous expression field learning, spot-level regional 

397 observation modeling, and multiscale conditional modulation within a unified 

398 framework, thereby jointly accounting for expression continuity, consistency with the 

399 measurement mechanism, and cross-scale morphology–molecule mapping 

400 capability. 

401 Experimental results demonstrate that HiFi-ST achieves overall competitive 

402 performance on the HER2+, cSCC, and Alex_NatGen datasets and outperforms 

403 baseline methods across most key metrics. In particular, the model shows consistent

It is The copyright holder for this preprint this version posted July 2, 2026. ;https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.06.29.735170 doi: bioRxiv preprint 23 /31

404 advantages in spatial correlation metrics (PCC and HEG_PCC), indicating improved 

405 recovery of spatial gene expression structure. Moreover, the learned continuous 

406 expression representations can support downstream spatial immunoanalysis tasks, 

407 such as assisted identification of candidate TLS regions, highlighting its potential 

408 value in tumor microenvironment analysis and spatial functional interpretation. 

409 Despite these promising results, several limitations remain. First, the current model 

410 performs continuous modeling only in two-dimensional tissue-section space and 

411 does not explicitly incorporate three-dimensional structural information. Second, the 

412 regional integration strategy relies on uniform sampling assumptions and does not 

413 yet leverage biologically informed, non-uniform priors such as nuclei density or tissue 

414 morphology constraints. Third, TLS analysis still depends on supervised annotations, 

415 which may limit scalability in fully unsupervised settings. Finally, the study does not 

416 report uncertainty estimates stratified by tissue sections or statistical significance 

417 testing across folds, which limits the statistical rigor of cross-method comparisons. 

418 Conclusion 

419 HiFi-ST provides a unified framework that elevates spatial transcriptomics from 

420 discrete spot-based observations to continuous expression field modeling. By 

421 bridging conditional neural fields with histology-guided multiscale representation 

422 learning, the model enables coherent reconstruction of spatial gene expression while 

423 maintaining consistency with the underlying measurement process.
