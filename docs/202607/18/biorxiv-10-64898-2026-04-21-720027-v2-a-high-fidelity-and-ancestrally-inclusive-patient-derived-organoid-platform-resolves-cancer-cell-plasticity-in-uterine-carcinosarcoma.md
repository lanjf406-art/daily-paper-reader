## 摘要
子宫癌肉瘤（UCS）是一种罕见但极其致命的子宫内膜癌，早期转移且对当前治疗方式耐药。它呈双相性，由恶性上皮细胞和间质细胞构成。基因组研究表明这些肿瘤是克隆性的，间质细胞通过癌细胞可塑性从上皮细胞产生。这一生物学特性难以研究，因为忠实的患者来源模型很少。不平等加剧了这一差距。非洲裔女性承担着最大的UCS负担，但在现有模型中代表性不足。为解决这一问题，我们从一个具有祖先包容性的UCS队列中建立了患者来源类器官（PDOs），同时建立了匹配的正常子宫内膜PDOs。类器官再现了原始肿瘤的双相组织学特征。在四个测序平台上，它们保留了肿瘤突变和拷贝数景观，在传代中保持稳定，并可持续扩增长达28个月。在单细胞分辨率下，UCS PDOs捕获了两个恶性区室，并追踪了沿上皮-间质转化轴的连续转录轨迹，捕获了患者特异性的癌细胞可塑性。这些模型也在概念验证治疗测试中提名了候选脆弱性。UCS PDOs富集了CREB家族转录程序，抑制CREB可降低其活力。联合抑制FGFR和YAP优于单独使用任一药物。总之，这项工作提供了一个组织学、基因组学和转录学上保真、具有祖先包容性且谱系解析的UCS类器官平台，用于研究一种侵袭性强且负担不平等的癌症中的癌细胞可塑性及其脆弱性。

## Abstract
Uterine carcinosarcoma (UCS) is a rare but extremely lethal endometrial cancer that metastasizes early and resists current treatment modalities. It is biphasic, built from malignant epithelial and mesenchymal cells. Genomic studies indicate that these tumors are clonal, and that the mesenchymal cells arise from the epithelial cells through cancer cell plasticity. This biology has been hard to study, because faithful patient-derived models are scarce. The gap is widened by inequity. Women of African ancestry carry the greatest burden of UCS, yet are underrepresented in existing models. To address this, we established patient-derived organoids (PDOs) from an ancestrally inclusive UCS cohort, alongside matched normal endometrial PDOs. The organoids reproduced the biphasic histology of the original tumors. Across four sequencing platforms, they retained the tumor mutation and copy-number landscape, remained stable across passages, and expanded for up to 28 months. At single-cell resolution, UCS PDOs captured both malignant compartments and traced continuous transcriptional trajectories along the epithelial-to-mesenchymal axis, capturing patient-specific cancer cell plasticity. The models also nominated candidate vulnerabilities in proof-of-concept therapeutic testing. UCS PDOs were enriched for CREB-family transcriptional programs, and CREB inhibition reduced their viability. Combined FGFR and YAP inhibition outperformed either agent alone. Together, this work delivers a histologically, genomically, and transcriptionally faithful, ancestrally inclusive, and lineage-resolved UCS organoid platform for studying cancer cell plasticity and its vulnerabilities in an aggressive and inequitably burdened cancer.
