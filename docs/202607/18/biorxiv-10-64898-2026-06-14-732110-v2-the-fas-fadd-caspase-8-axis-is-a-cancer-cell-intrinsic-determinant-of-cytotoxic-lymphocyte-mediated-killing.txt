Abstract: 15 

Cytotoxic lymphocytes induce cancer cell death through death receptor –ligand interactions and 16 

the perforin –granzyme pathway. These pathways are generally thought to converge on the 17 

activation of executioner caspases to drive apoptosis. Here, we employed a reductionist 18 

approach to systematically disrupt key cell death mediators in a cytotoxic lymphocyte killing 19 

system to define their roles in determining cancer cell fate. We found that loss of executioner 20 

caspases conferred only limited resistance to cytotoxic lymphocyte –mediated killing. To 21 

identify cancer cell –intrinsic regulators that function beyond executioner caspases, we 22 

performed unbiased genome -wide CRISPR screens in executioner caspase -deficient cells. 23

Unexpectedly, disruption of Fas or FADD —core components of the death receptor pathway —24 

conferred substantial resistance to cytotoxic lymphocyte –mediated killing even in the absence 25 

of executioner caspases. This resistance persisted following additional disruption of known 26 

downstream mediators of Fas –FADD –caspase -8 (CASP8) signaling. Together, these findings 27 

identify the Fas –FADD –CASP8 axis as a central cancer cell –intrinsic determinant of 28 

susceptibility to cytotoxic lymphocyte –mediated killing whose function is not fully explained 29 

by canonical apoptotic or non -apoptotic effector pathways. Our results further suggest that 30 

CASP8 engages additional downstream substrates or mechanisms to promote cytotoxic 31 

lymphocyte –induced cancer cell death. 32 

Introduction 33 

Cytotoxic lymphocytes, including cytotoxic T lymphocytes (CTLs) and natural killer (NK) cells, 34 

play fundamental roles in the elimination of cancer cells.  These immune effector cells recognize 35 

malignant targets through distinct mechanisms: CTLs detect tumor –derived antigens presented 36 

on major histocompatibility complex (MHC) molecules  1, whereas NK cells sense aberrant 37 

expression of activating and inhibitory ligands  2. However,  both CTLs and NK cells induce 38 

target  cell death primarily through two major pathways  3,4 . The first is the death ligand –death 39 

receptor pathway, most prominently mediated by the interaction of Fas ligand (FasL) expressed 40 

on CTLs with the Fas receptor on target cells. The second  pathway  involves the delivery of 41 

cytotoxic granzymes into target cells through perforin –dependent pore formation in the target 42 

cell plasma membrane. The ability of cytotoxic lymphocytes to selectively eradicate malignant 43 

cells underpins modern cancer immunotherapy, including chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T -44 

cell therapy, T -cell receptor (TCR) -engineered T -cell therapy, and  NK cell –based therapeutic 45 

approaches  5-7.46

A key downstream consequence of both the death receptor and perforin –granzyme pathways is 47 

the activation of apoptosis, a well -characterized form of programmed cell death mediated by 48 

caspases  8. Among these, caspase -3 (CASP3)  serves as a principal executioner caspase in 49 

apoptosis  9,10 . It can be activated directly by granzyme B  11  or via caspase -8 (CASP8) 50 

downstream of Fas –FADD signaling  12,13 . In addition, both  CASP3  and granzyme B cleave B ID 51 

into truncated BID  (tB ID ) 14 -16 , which promotes mitochondrial outer membrane 52 

permeabilization (MOMP) through B AX  and/or B AK  17,18 . This process results in the release 53 

of cytochrome c and Smac/DIABLO, facilitating apoptosome formation and caspase -9 (CASP9) 54 

activation, which ultimately leads to  CASP3  activation  19 -22 . Activated  CASP3  executes the 55 

apoptotic program by cleaving numerous downstream substrates  9, resulting in hallmark 56 

features such as apoptotic body formation and DNA fragmentation  8,23 . Two other executioner 57 

caspases, caspase -6 (CASP6) and caspase -7 (CASP7), also exist.  CASP3 and CASP7 show 58 

functional redundancy in driving apoptosis in response to both extrinsic and intrinsic apoptotic 59 

stimuli, whereas CASP6  has  no apparent role in these processes  24 .60 

In addition to apoptosis, alternative forms of cell death have been implicated in cytotoxic 61 

lymphocyte –mediated killing of cancer cells  25 . Pyroptosis , a lytic form of programmed cell 62 

death mediated by pore -forming gasdermin proteins  26,27 , can be induced in target cancer cells 63 

by cytotoxic lymphocytes  28,29 . For example, gasdermin  E (GSDME) can be cleaved and 64 

activated by  CASP3  27,30 ; t herefore, in GSDME -expressing cancer cells,  CASP3  can mediate 65 

both apoptosis and pyroptosis via the CASP3 –GSDME axis. Gasdermins have also been 66 

reported as direct substrates of granzymes  28,31 . Furthermore, in cases  where  CASP 8 is inhibited, 67 

death receptor  signaling  can initiate necroptosis, an alternative signaling pathway  mediated by 68 

receptor -interacting protein kinase 1 –receptor -interacting protein kinase 3 –mixed lineage 69 

kinase domain -like (RIPK1 –RIPK3 –MLKL) axis  32 . Ferroptosis is another form of regulated 70

Despite our increased understanding of the various forms of cell death and their associated 73 

pathways, the contribution of individual pathways and mediators to cancer cell survival and 74 

resistance remains poorly understood . Indeed, although executioner caspases play critical roles 75 

in apoptosis and are essential for apoptotic hallmarks such as DNA fragmentation, they have 76 

often been reported to be dispensable for cell death itself  15,35 -37 . In such cases, cell death is 77 

frequently attributed to alternative mechanisms, including pyroptosis, necroptosis, and 78 

mitochondrial damage resulting from MOMP  31,38 . However, a systematic evaluation of the 79 

relative contributions of alternative cell death pathways and their mediators has been lacking. 80 

Here, we employed an unbiased approach to identify cancer cell –intrinsic factors that determine 81 

susceptibility to cytotoxic lymphocyte –mediated killing. We found that cancer cells deficient 82 

in Fas –FADD –CASP8 signaling are markedly more resistant to cytotoxic lymphocyte –83 

mediated killing , even in the absence of executioner caspases, necroptosis, or BID –mediated 84 

mitochondrial damage.  Our findings establish Fas –FADD –CASP8 signaling as a central cancer 85 

cell –intrinsic determinant of cell fate under immune attack. Furthermore, our results strongly 86 

indicate the existence of additional, as yet unidentified substrates downstream of Fas –FADD –87 

CASP8 that contribute to cytotoxic lymphocyte –induced cancer cell death. 88

Discussion 237 

In this study, we used targeted genetic disruption to evaluate the contribution of multiple known 238 

death mediators involved in apoptosis, pyroptosis, necroptosis, and MOMP to cytotoxic 239 

lymphocyte –induced cancer cell death. In parallel, we performed unbiased genome -wide 240 

CRISPR screens to systematically identify cancer cell –intrinsic factors that determine survival 241 

or death following cytotoxic lymphocyte attack. Compared with the most reductionist 242

Various methodologies have been developed to measure cell death, and care must be taken when 253 

interpreting their readouts, as many approaches have intrinsic limitations. For example, 254 

CASP3/7 substrates are frequently used as indicators of cancer cell death; however, this 255 

approach inherently detects only apoptosis or CASP3 –GSDME –mediated pyroptosis and 256 

therefore excludes other forms of cell death. Similarly, methods that rely on membrane 257 

permeabilization, such as calcein release assays, lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) release assays, 258 

and intracellular DNA staining, tend to emphasize necrotic forms of death, because plasma 259 

membrane integrity is generally preserved during apoptosis, at least in its early stages  50 .260 

Viability assays based on ATP quantification or cellular metabolic activity, such as CellTiter -261 

Glo and MTT assays, are difficult to apply in co -culture systems because the presence of 262 

effector immune cells confounds interpretation of the measurements.  In this study, we adopted 263 

an unbiased approach in which cancer cell death or expansion was quantified based on 264 

endogenously expressed fluorescent signals. Because  dead  cancer cells cease fluorescent 265 

protein expression and eventually lose fluorescence, whereas surviving cells continue to 266

Although the extrinsic death signaling pathway mediated by Fas –FADD –CASP8 has been 269 

recognized for many years, its importance has primarily been appreciated in the context of 270 

apoptosis through downstream activation of the executioner caspases CASP3 and CASP7  51,52 ,271 

either directly by CASP8 or indirectly  via  CASP8 –mediated cleavage of BID  53 . Our data 272 

suggest that the Fas –FADD –CASP8 axis is more broadly important than previously appreciated, 273 

remaining functionally relevant even when canonical downstream substrates, including CASP3, 274 

CASP7, and BID, are fully disrupted. 275 

Notably, CASP3, despite being a central mediator of apoptosis and an upstream regulator of 276 

GSDME –mediated pyroptosis, is rarely mutated in cancer  54,55 . This somewhat counterintuitive 277 

observation raises the possibility that executioner caspases are not the ultimate determinants of 278 

cell death per  se but  rather function as downstream effectors that channel cells into specific 279 

forms of death following activation of upstream death signals. Caspase –independent cell death 280 

has long been recognized  35 , and MOMP has been proposed to represent a point of no return for 281 

cell  death , irrespective of downstream caspase activation  48 . Nevertheless, CTLs have been 282 

shown to abrogate target cell proliferation and induce cell death even upon inhibition of both 283 

executioner caspases and mitochondrial events  37 . This finding is consistent with our 284 

observation that systematic disruption of several well -established mediators of cell death —285 

including  CASP3 /7 (apoptosis and pyroptosis),  MLKL  (necroptosis), and  BID  (MOMP) —did 286 

not fully  prevent  cytotoxic lymphocyte –induced killing. Importantly, even when these 287 

downstream death mediators were disrupted, additional deletion of upstream Fas –FADD –288 

CASP8 components conferred substantial resistance to cytotoxic lymphocyte –mediated killing. 289 

Consistent with this, analysis of TCGA data shows that alterations in  CASP8  are more 290 

frequently observed in cancer than mutations in  CASP3 , CASP7 , or  BID . It is noteworthy that 291

Together, our findings from cytotoxic lymphocyte killing assays, genome -wide CRISPR 298 

screens, and clinical observations strongly indicate that additional, as yet unidentified, CASP8 299 

substrates or downstream effectors contribute to cytotoxic lymphocyte –induced cancer cell 300 

death. 301 

In summary, using a reductionist genetic approach, we provide compelling evidence that the 302 

Fas –FADD –CASP8 signaling pathway governs cancer cell susceptibility to cytotoxic 303 

lymphocyte –mediated killing. The relevance of this pathway extends beyond its established 304 

roles in apoptosis, necroptosis, and mitochondrial damage. Our work offers a potential 305 

explanation for why certain well -established death mediators, such as  CASP3 , are rarely 306 

mutated in cancer. These findings also highlight the importance of future studies aimed at 307 

identifying previously unrecognized CASP8 substrates that may contribute to cancer cell  death 308 

beyond currently defined canonical cell death pathways. 309
