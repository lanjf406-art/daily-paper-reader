Abstract 1

Upon treatment, cancer cells engage non-genetic adaptations, including tolerance and subsequent 2

persistence, to survive therapy. Eliciting programmed cancer cell death in these persister cells (PCs) 3

remains a primary goal in oncology. We found that ferroptosis is the programmed cell death 4

mechanism most deregulated in persisters by some of the most widely used therapeutic regimens, 5

including platinum-based therapies, which combined with ferroptosis inducers ablate persister 6

colorectal cancer cells. Conversely, persisters emerging from topoisomerase inhibitor regimens 7

withstand ferroptosis and ferroptotic inducers, increasing instead intracellular iron concentration. We 8

found that topoisomerase inhibitors trigger the Xc - antiporter axis ( via SLC7A11 and CD44) 9

increasing both intracellular cystine, to activate GPX4, and extracellular glutamate. Glutamate then 10 

engages the NMDA receptors (NMDARs), which are essential in neurotransmission but recently 11 

reported to be deregulated also in cancer cells. In PCs, NMDARs stimulate intracellular Ca 2+ uptake 12 

and trigger the AKT/NFE2L2 axis, thereby engaging a cytoprotective program to cope with oxidative 13 

stress. Furthermore, we found that NFE2L2 increases the distance between the endoplasmic reticulum 14 

and mitochondria while reducing mitochondrial ROS in PCs. The synergistic inhibition of both the 15 

standard (Xc - antiporter) and this novel NMDAR/NFE2L2 axis resensitizes PCs to ferroptosis. These 16 

data provide new opportunities to improve the efficacy of widely used therapeutic regimens. 17

Introduction 22 

Cancer cells exposed to chemotherapy enter in a state of tolerance, a response driven by 23 

autophagy that allows them to withstand even exceedingly high drug concentrations 1. Upon 24 

prolonged drug exposure, tolerance morphs into a persistence state, an alternative form of non-genetic 25 

adaptation contributing to the emergence of resistance 1. Persister cells (PCs) are slow-cycling and 26 

undergo specific metabolic and epigenetic reprogramming to survive therapy, which includes 27 

chromatin remodelling 2, aldehyde dehydrogenase (ALDH) activity or fatty acid oxidation 28 

enhancement 3-4 and maintenance of mitochondrial fitness through PINK1-dependent mitophagy 1.29 

The eradication of PCs remains one of the main aims in cancer therapy 5. Cells may undergo 30 

regulated cell death (RCD) through several pathways, including apoptosis, necroptosis, autophagy, 31 

pyroptosis, ferroptosis, and cuproptosis 6. However, PCs manage to evade these regulated 32 

mechanisms. For example, following targeted and conventional cancer therapies, PCs elude both 33 

intrinsic and extrinsic pathways of apoptosis 7-8 . As a result, there is growing interest in triggering 34 

and exploiting alternative RCD mechanisms to eliminate PCs in cancer 9.35 

Ferroptosis is a form of RCD elicited by the accumulation of intracellular free iron, which 36 

activates pro-oxidative enzymes, alters mitochondrial activity and tilt the balance from 37 

monounsaturated (MUFA) towards polyunsaturated fatty acid (PUFA). The net result is the 38 

peroxidation of lipids on the plasma membrane followed by cellular demise 10 . Compounds eliciting 39 

ferroptosis have been devised that overcome resistance to traditional therapies and enhance the impact 40 

of radiotherapy and immunotherapy 11 . However, cancer cells mitigate the threat of overwhelming 41 

membrane lipid peroxidation leveraging the concerted activity of several enzymes and metabolites. 42 

These strategies involve various antioxidant defence mechanisms, including cystine-GPX4 (Xc -

43

the plasma membrane. Cystine then increases the synthesis of glutathione (GSH), a reducing agent 46 

used by glutathione peroxidase 4 (GPX4) to convert toxic phospholipid hydroperoxides into non-47

Here, we explored which RCD path is triggered in PCs following chemotherapy, which 51

Results 54 

FOLFOX- but not FOLFIRI-PCs are susceptible to ferroptosis induction 55 

We focused on colorectal cancer (CRC), a major cancer type with limited therapeutic 56 

strategies, where cells enter a persistence state following chemotherapy 1,15-16 . To determine whether 57 

CRC PCs are susceptible to any form of RCD after treatment with standard of care, we probed the 58 

pathways responsible for the various forms of cell death in PCs derived from the CRC cell line 59 

HCT116 treated with the two standard-of care treatments for CRC 17 , FOLFOX (5-fluorouracil + 60 

oxaliplatin) or FOLFIRI (5-fluorouracil + SN38). We assessed the variation in gene expression on 61 

pathways associated with the different forms of RCD, leveraging our recently published single cell 62 

RNA-seq (scRNA-seq) data 1. With the prominent exception of ferroptosis, we did not detect any 63 

deregulation in gene expression of various RCD programs in PCs, including apoptosis, which was 64 

also assessed via Annexin V labelling (Fig.1 a, Suppl. Fig. 1a-b) 1. Instead, we found a strong increase 65 

in the expression of ferroptosis-related genes (Fig. 1a). Prompted by these findings, we anticipated a 66 

strong synergistic response between standard chemotherapy treatments and ferroptosis inducers such 67 

as Erastin and RSL3, which respectively inhibit the cystine-glutamate antiporter system Xc − and 68 

glutathione peroxidase 4 (GPX4) 18-19 . Both Erastin and RSL3 almost eliminated PCs after FOLFOX 69 

treatment (Fig. 1b), suggesting that indeed this treatment is associated with an enhanced, potentially 70 

exploitable vulnerability to ferroptosis. 71

Despite a similar increase in ferroptosis-related genes (Fig. 1a), FOLFIRI treatment associated 72 

with ferroptosis inducers failed to show any effect (Fig. 1b). Since iron (Fe 2+ ) levels are directly 73 

linked to the induction of ferroptosis, we anticipated that FOLFIRI treatment would be associated 74 

with reduced intracellular Fe 2+ levels. Surprisingly, however, labile Fe 2+ content was significantly 75 

higher in PCs treated with FOLFIRI than with FOLFOX (Fig. 1c and Suppl. Fig. 1c). Additionally, 76 

in FOLFIRI-treated cells, this accumulation of Fe 2+ was associated with a significantly more 77 

pronounced increase in ferritin (FTH1), and curiously in transferrin receptor (TFRC), markers of Fe 2+ 

78 

storage and uptake, respectively (Suppl. Fig. 1d), when compared with FOLFOX-treated cells. Taken 79 

together, these data demonstrate that FOLFIRI treatment confers resistance to ferroptosis even in the 80 

presence of a robust increase in cellular Fe 2+ . This accumulation, likely mediated by deregulated 81 

TFRC-driven extracellular transport, highlights a clear uncoupling of Fe 2+ , FTH1, and TFRC within 82 

the homeostatic regulatory network 20 .83 

Previous evidence has suggested that Fe 2+ may be required for cancer stem-like cells and 84 

tumour-initiating cells survival 21 . Nevertheless, how Fe 2+ functionally contributes to the maintenance 85 

of a treatment-resistant phenotype is still not fully elucidated. We then hypothesized that to withstand 86 

FOLFIRI, PCs rely on Fe 2+ accumulation. FOLFIRI-pretreated cells were challenged with the potent 87 

Fe 2+ chelator deferoxamine (DFO), which acts by sequestering intracellular iron 22 . Treatment with 88 

DFO markedly impaired the viability of PCs while promoting an increase in Propidium Iodide (PI)-89 

positive cells (Fig. 1d and Suppl. Fig. 1e), thus validating the hypothesis that Fe 2+ , despite its role in 90 

triggering ferroptosis, in FOLFIRI-PCs exerts nonetheless a pro-survival function. 91

phospholipids, thereby initiating peroxidation 10 . We then determined the levels of lipid peroxidation 107

We hence explored whether the resistance towards ferroptosis in FOLFIRI-PCs might be related to 113 

an increased activity of this system. Indeed, FOLFIRI-PCs showed a robust increase of both NADPH, 114 

which provides reducing equivalents to regenerate GSH 23 , and GSH per se , which were instead not 115 

enhanced in FOLFOX-PCs (Fig. 2b-c). Also GPX4, a powerful antioxidant enzyme belonging to the 116 

glutathione peroxidase family 24 , was strongly upregulated in FOLFIRI- but not in FOLFOX-PCs (Fig. 117 

2d), in line with previous observation that Xc --mediated cystine uptake promotes not only GSH, but 118 

also GPX4 protein synthesis 25 .119 

In all, these results suggest that, despite a strong increase in cellular Fe 2+ , FOLFIRI-PCs 120 

manage to bypass ferroptosis through the activation of the Xc - antiporter system, leading to the 121 

increase of GSH biosynthesis and of GPX4 26 .122 

Unlike FOLFOX, the FOLFIRI regimen includes SN38, the active metabolite of the 123 

topoisomerase inhibitor irinotecan. Treating cancer cells with SN38 alone had similar effects than 124 

FOLFIRI (Suppl. Fig. 2a). Furthermore, the co-treatment of SN38 and ferroptosis inducers failed to 125 

alter the survival of PCs, consistent with the results obtained using FOLFIRI (Suppl. Fig 2b). SN38 126 

interferes with DNA unwinding and induces DNA double strand breaks. Accordingly, cancer cells 127

accessibility 27 . We wanted to determine first whether PCs treated with FOLFIRI demonstrated a 129 

different level of chromatin compaction, and next if the expression of specific genes was associated 130 

with the treatment. To this end, we exploited single cell GET-Seq (scGET-Seq), a technology that 131 

probes both open and closed chromatin, at the single cell level 28 . We have previously shown that upon 132

Glutamate activates NMDAR and induces cytoprotection 171 

Since the Xc - antiporter couples cystine uptake with glutamate efflux, we investigated whether 172 

glutamate extruded via this axis could protect PCs from ferroptosis. Consistent with this hypothesis, 173 

we observed a substantial increase in extracellular glutamate levels in FOLFIRI-PCs relative to 174 

untreated cells and FOLFOX-PCs. (Fig. 3a). 175 

Glutamate is an excitatory neurotransmitter that triggers the activation of N-methyl-D-176 

aspartate receptors (NMDARs), ushering calcium (Ca 2+ ) from the extracellular milieu to the 177 

cytoplasm 31 . NMDARs are expressed not only in the central nervous system (CNS) cells, but have 178 

been reported also in several cancer types, where their role remains poorly understood 32 . In fact, based 179 

on the Human Protein Atlas (https://www.proteinatlas.org), NMDARs (GRIN1, GRIN2B, C, D, 180 

GRIN3B and GRINA) are expressed also in colon cancer and in the HCT116 CRC cells. As a proxy 181 

of the NMDAR activity, we quantified the intracellular Ca 2+ content by flow cytometry analysis 33 ,182 

in the presence of the NMDARs inhibitors Memantine or Dizocilpine. FOLFIRI-PCs presented a 183 

higher content of Ca 2+ than parental cells and FOLFOX-PCs (Fig. 3b). This increase was blunted by 184 

the treatment with both inhibitors (Fig. 3c), suggesting that FOLFIRI increases Ca 2+ flux through the 185 

engagement of NMDARs. 186 

We next asked which might be the consequences of the increase in Ca 2+ in FOLFIRI-PCs. The 187 

concentration of Ca²⁺ in the cytoplasm is tightly regulated, and changes in Ca²⁺ concentration activate 188 

signalling events, including Protein kinase C (PKC), mitogen-activated MAPK kinases (p38 and 189 

p42/44-MAPK), and the phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K)/AKT axis 34 . To gain insights on which 190 

pathways may be triggered by the increased intracellular Ca 2+ , we explored bulk RNA-seq data, 191 

surveying the genes specifically upregulated upon FOLFIRI treatment in PCs (Suppl. Table 1). 192 

Among the 708 genes emerging from this analysis, several downstream targets of the PI3K/AKT 193 

pathway appeared (Fig. 3d). The activation of the PI3K/AKT pathway was confirmed in our previous 194 

FOLFIRI-PCs single-cell RNA-seq data 1 (Fig. 3e). This was further validated by western blot 195

NMDARs increases Ca 2+ uptake, which in turn triggers PI3K/AKT and the ferroptosis inhibitor 207 

NFE2L2 39 . To substantiate this model, we treated PCs with the NMDARs inhibitors and assessed the 208 

levels of p-AKT and NFE2L2 proteins. Indeed, both inhibitors reduced pAKT and NFE2L2 levels 209 

(Fig. 3g). Moreover, the combination of NMDARs inhibitors with FOLFIRI increased lipid 210 

peroxidation (Fig. 3h) but had no effect on cell viability (Fig. 3i). These results reinforce the notion 211 

that multiple anti-oxidative mechanisms cooperate after FOLFIRI exposure; consequently, alternative 212 

or combined approaches are necessary to trigger ferroptosis in FOLFIRI-PCs. 213

NFE2L2 inhibition resensitizes PCs to ferroptosis 235 

How does NFE2L2 drive an antioxidant effect to protect PCs from peroxidation-dependent 236 

cell death? To answer this question, we performed an RNA-seq analysis in NFE2L2 silencing 237 

conditions. Beyond the significant down regulation of known target genes, such as NQO1 (NADPH 238 

quinone dehydrogenase 1) and genes involved in pentose phosphate pathway (i.e. TKT, TALDO1 and 239 

G6PD) 40 (Suppl. Fig. 3a), we were surprised to find the upregulation of Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER) 240 

stress and Unfolded Protein Response (UPR – PERK/ATF4) genes (Suppl. Fig. 3b). 241 

It has been recently reported that the junction between ER and mitochondria (M), namely EM 242 

Contact (EMC), modulates lipid peroxidation and ferroptosis and that ER stress and UPR are tightly 243 

involved in EMCs regulation 41 . The close proximity between ER and M (thus, reduced distance) 244 

favours ferroptosis, while an increased space between these two compartments protects cells 41 . The 245 

molecular mechanism regulating EMC remains elusive. 246 

We analysed transmitted electron microscopy (TEM) images in PCs and measured ER-M 247 

distance. FOLFOX treatment profoundly reduced ER-M distance, much more than FOLFIRI, as the 248 

gap separating the ER and mitochondria shortened from an average of 17 nm in untreated cells to 249 

6 nm in response to FOLFOX and only to 12 nm in FOLFIRI-PCs (Suppl. Fig. 3c). 250

Mitochondrial ROS precipitate ferroptosis induced by Xc - antiporter inhibition 312 

Our findings demonstrate that FOLFIRI-PCs trigger two anti-ferroptotic programs. One 313 

pathway depends on the recruitment of the cystine-glutamate antiporter Xc - which activates the 314 

GSH/GPX4 cascade. However, targeting this system alone fails to sensitize cells to ferroptosis 315 

because of the parallel NMDARs/NFE2L2/mitochondria axis. Through NFE2L2 regulation, 316 

FOLFIRI-PCs restrict mitochondrial impairment and control oxidative stress to support survival. 317 

Iron is a vital catalyst in this process. Free intracellular iron both reacts with hydrogen 318 

peroxides via the Fenton reaction to generate highly reactive hydroxyl radicals, which rapidly 319 

accelerate the oxidation of lipids 45 and plays a critical role serving as a fundamental component in 320 

the biosynthesis of heme and iron−sulfur (Fe−S) clusters, which serve as crucial cofactors within the 321 

mitochondrial respiratory chain 46 . Nonetheless, a striking accumulation of Fe 2+ was observed in 322 

FOLFIRI-PCs and this increase promoted survival rather than cell death (Fig. 1d). We hypothesized 323 

that in FOLFIRI-PCs, most of the Fe 2+ is directed toward mitochondria to sustain organelles fitness, 324 

essential for cell survival. To test this hypothesis, we first evaluated mitochondrial Fe 2+ levels using 325 

Mito-FerroGreen, a fluorescent probe that detects Fe 2+ , co-stained with MitoBright, a mitochondria-326 

retained dye. We confirmed that FOLFIRI-PCs exhibited a marked increase in mitochondrial Fe 2+

Sulfasalazine in combination with NFE2L2 inhibition ablates FOLFIRI-PCs 359 

Although Erastin and RSL3 have been used to study the induction of ferroptosis in vitro and 360 

in vivo , they never reached the clinics, due to toxic effects recorded in preclinical studies 47-48 .361 

We then evaluated Sulfasalazine, an anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory agent 362 

approved for chronic inflammatory diseases 49 and recently investigated in clinical trials for metastatic 363 

colorectal cancer, glioblastoma, and acute leukaemia. Furthermore, Sulfasalazine has been 364 

repurposed as an inhibitor of the Xc - antiporter system to trigger ferroptosis 50-51 . Sulfasalazine, at a 365 

dose reducing survival by 30% of HCT116 parental cell, effectively increased lipid peroxidation 366 

(Suppl. Fig. 5a). We then tested the effect of sulfasalazine in the context of CRC persistence. In line 367 

with the results obtained with Erastin and RSL3, Sulfasalazine was effective in ablating FOLFOX-368 

PCs (Fig. 6a), while having no effect in FOLFIRI-PCs (Fig. 6b). The combination of sulfasalazine 369 

with the specific NFE2L2 inhibitor ML385 resensitized FOLFIRI-PCs and strongly reduced cell 370 

viability (Fig. 6c). 371

Discussion 418 

Tumour cell death is the main goal of most anti-cancer therapies 58 , as well as eradication of 419 

tumour reservoirs, such as persister cells 59 . In this study, we show that ferroptosis is strongly activated 420 

during chemotherapy-induced persistence of colorectal cancer cells, with platinum-based 421 

combination sensitizing persister cells to ferroptosis induction. Surprisingly, following 422 

topoisomerase inhibition, persister cells were instead resistant to the induction of ferroptosis. In these 423 

cells, CD44 cooperates with SLC7A11, the main subunit of the Xc - antiporter system, increasing 424 

cystine uptake and activating the GPX4 antioxidant pathway. Notably, blocking the Xc - system, by 425 

Erastin or the GPX4 inhibitor RSL3, was not sufficient to sensitize cells to ferroptosis, suggesting the 426 

involvement of an additional mechanism triggering resistance. We found an increase of the 427 

extracellular glutamate which, in turn, stimulates the NMDARs to activate the downstream NFE2L2 428 

signalling axis which, by directly maintaining ER-mitochondrial distance, mitigates oxidative stress. 429 

Ultimately, targeting both axes simultaneously is essential to overcome this defence mechanism, 430 

induce ferroptosis, and clear the persister cell population. 431 

The role of the synaptic plasticity receptors NMDARs in cancer is starting to emerge. 432 

NMDARs exert a central role in the synaptic transmission and plasticity 60 . However, recent evidence 433 

suggests that these receptors are expressed in tissues of different tumour types, such as pancreatic 61-

, small cell lung 63 and breast cancer 64 . Aberrant accumulation of glutamate in the extracellular 435 

compartment activates glutamate receptors on cancer cells, promoting malignant growth 65 . NMDARs 436 

stimulate several tumorigenic pathways, such as mTOR, ERK1/2, and CREB through Ca 2+ uptake 32 .437

We propose that NMDARs, when activated by glutamate binding, mediate ferroptosis resistance in 438 

response to topoisomerase inhibitor treatments. Our finding is consistent with existing data that 439 

correlate these classical synaptic receptors with rituximab resistance in neurological disorders such 440 

as autoimmune encephalitis or Ca 2+ -dependent excitotoxicity in epilepsy 66-67 . In addition, NMDARs 441 

have been associated with refractory to temozolomide chemotherapy in glioblastoma patients, due to 442 

the increase of the DNA repair enzyme, O6-methylguanine DNA methyltransferase 68 . Our data 443 

demonstrate that NMDARs, by activating the Ca 2+ -dependent survival signals, such as AKT 444 

phosphorylation, trigger the NFE2L2 transcription factor in the context of cancer persistence. 445 

Endoplasmic reticulum-mitochondria contact (EMC) is emerging as the central hub in 446 

mitochondrial ROS production and for the oxidative ferroptotic hotspot 41 . Several proteins, by acting 447 

as membrane tethers, have been described to maintain EMC 69 . However, the driving factors behind 448 

this regulation remain poorly defined. Our finding sheds light on the role of NFE2L2 in triggering 449 

this process. We hypothesize that NFE2L2 controls ER stress and the unfolded protein response and, 450 

by maintaining the distance among organelles, halts both lipid peroxidation and mitochondrial 451 

damage. Such a complex regulation confers resistance to ferroptosis and opens new avenues for the 452 

characterization of the mechanisms governing EMC, also beyond the setting of persistence. 453 

Targeting iron metabolism, either through Fe 2+ deprivation or by leveraging Fe 2+ accumulation 454 

to trigger ferroptosis, represents a promising therapeutic strategy in oncology 70 . As such, increasing 455 

cellular Fe 2+ uptake is a well-established strategy to induce ferroptosis, also in persister cells 30,71 .456 

Conversely, the potential protective role of Fe 2+ in PCs has not been previously investigated to the 457 

best of our knowledge. Indeed, despite its role in driving ferroptosis, Fe 2+ could alternatively promote 458 

cancer cell growth, a phenomenon already documented in signalling pathways associated with other 459 

forms of programmed cell death 72-73 . Here, we demonstrate that following FOLFIRI treatment, Fe 2+ 

460 

chelation ablates PCs. In several settings Fe 2+ chelation has been proposed as a strategy to treat 461 

cancers, for example in colorectal, ovarian and hepatocellular carcinoma, glioblastoma and 462

Why do these PCs require iron? We argue that Fe 2+ in this setting is required for mitochondrial 465 

fitness. In particular, we delineate the role of Fe 2+ in maintaining mitochondrial homeostasis, which 466 

is critical for tumour initiation and growth 74 . Indeed, beyond the classical role in the Fenton reaction, 467 

which generates highly reactive hydroxyl radicals and drives lipid peroxidation 75 , Fe 2+ can be 468 

exploited by cancer cells to sustain mitochondrial Fe-S cluster and heme synthesis 76 . In this study, we 469 

propose that this distinctive mitochondrial mechanism acts as an essential player in sustaining cell 470 

survival during treatment and cancer persistence. To support this hypothesis, we speculate that 471 

NFE2L2, by modulating the labile Fe 2+ pool via FTH1 regulation, ensures an adequate iron supply 472 

while preventing excess Fe 2+ from initiating detrimental Fenton reactions 77 . Conversely, an inability 473 

of cancer cells to compartmentalize Fe 2+ triggers a massive surge in mitochondrial reactive oxygen 474 

species. Consequently, employing an inhibitor of Fe 2+ transport to mitochondria, or utilizing a 475 

combination of drugs that deprives the cancer cell of mitochondria protective mechanisms, forces it 476 

into rapid, catastrophic, iron-dependent cell death. 477 

Exploiting the mechanistic links between chemotherapy and iron-dependent pathways during 478 

the persistent state provides a promising framework for developing synergistic combination regimens. 479 

Accordingly, we suggest that ferroptotic induction could represent a successful strategy in 480 

combination with FOLFOX. Conversely, exploiting the ferroptotic pathway after topoisomerase 481 

inhibitor treatments is more challenging, as it requires the simultaneous inhibition of both antioxidant 482 

strategies, namely the Xc - antiporter system and the NMDAR/NFE2L2 axis, to dismantle the 483 

antioxidant response and eradicate FOLFIRI persister cells. 484 

How to safely induce ferroptosis for cancer treatment remains a critical question, as the 485 

primary challenge lies in developing a therapeutic strategy that protects healthy cells while selectively 486 

targeting tumour cells, alongside identifying patients who would most benefit from ferroptosis-based 487 

interventions 78 . To address this, we successfully utilized Sulfasalazine, an FDA-approved anti-488

targeted therapy against NFE2L2 to overcome drug resistance, validating these findings in a highly 492 

clinically relevant model, such as colorectal cancer patient-derived organoids that recapitulate the 493 

FOLFIRI-induced activation of the antioxidant response. 494 

In conclusion, we propose exploiting iron-dependent mechanisms to eradicate persister cells 495 

in colorectal cancer, while also accounting for the diversity of the plastic response to therapy as an 496 

intrinsic property of these cells. Alternative approaches must be tailored to successfully target survival 497 

machineries during the persistent state. Furthermore, additional analyses are required to develop 498 

patient stratification strategies, identifying individuals who would benefit most from ferroptosis 499
