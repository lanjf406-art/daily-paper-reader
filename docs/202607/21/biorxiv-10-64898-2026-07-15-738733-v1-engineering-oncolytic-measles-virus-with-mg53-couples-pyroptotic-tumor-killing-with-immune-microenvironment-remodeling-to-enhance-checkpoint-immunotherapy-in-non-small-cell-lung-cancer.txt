Abstract 26 

Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer -related mortality worldwide, with only approximately 30% of patients 27 

benefiting from current immune checkpoint immunotherapy. Oncolytic virotherapy offers a promising strategy to 28 

overcome this resistance ; however, achieving both potent tumor cytotoxicity and robust antitumor immun e29 

activation remains a major challenge. Here, we engineered oncolytic measles virus expressing the tumor 30 

suppressor MG53 (TRIM72) , designated rMeV -MG 53, and evaluated its therapeutic potential against non -small 31 

cell lung cancer (NSCLC). rMeV -MG53 retained replication kinetics comparable to parental MeV while driving 32 

significantly greater cytotoxicity than an unarmed control in NSCLC cell s. Mechanistically, MG53 arming 33 

amplified caspase -3–dependent apoptosis and potentiated Gasdermin E (GSDME) cleavage, engaging 34 

GSDME -mediated pyroptosis as a key tumor -killing mechanism ; pharmacological inhibition confirmed caspase -35 

dependent apoptosis as the initiating event leading to pyroptosis, while excluding necroptosis and ferroptosis as 36 

significant contributors to rMeV -MG53 -induced cytotoxicity . MG53 overexpression induced an intrinsic pro -37 

inflammatory transcriptional signature enriched for TNF, NF -κB, and IL -17 signaling, and rMeV -MG53 elicited 38 

significantly stronger type I interferon and pro -inflammatory cytokine induction than the unarmed control. In a n39 

immunocompetent , MeV -permissive syngeneic NSCLC model, intratumoral rMeV -MG53 achieved superior 40 

tumor growth inhibition, amplified caspase -3/GSDME pyroptosis, selectively upregulated Cxcl10 , Ifng , and Il1b ,41 

and drove robust CD8 ⁺ T-cell and Granzyme B ⁺ effector infiltration. This immune remodeling was accompanied 42 

by compensatory PD -L1 upregulation, and combining rMeV -MG53 with anti -PD -L1 blockade achieved the 43 

strongest tumor suppression of all treatment groups. These findings establish MG53 -armed oncolytic MeV as a 44 

strategy coupling GSDME -mediated pyroptosis with antitumor immune remodeling to sensitize immune -resistant 45 

NSCLC to checkpoint immunotherapy. 46

Introduction 50 

Lung cancer remains the leading cause of cancer -related mortality worldwide, responsible for approximately 2.5 51 

million new diagnoses and 1.8 million deaths annually as of 2022 (1) . Non -small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) 52 

accounts for approximately 85% of all lung cancer cases, encompassing adenocarcinoma, squamous cell 53 

carcinoma, and large cell carcinoma as major histological subtypes (2) . Despite considerable advances in 54 

targeted therapy and immunotherapy, the five -year overall survival rate for lung cancer remains approximately 55 

25%, underscoring the persistent need for novel therapeutic strategies (3) . The advent of immunotherapy such 56 

as immune checkpoint inhibitors (IC Is) has revolutionized the treatment landscape for advanced NSCLC, with 57 

response rates of 10% -25% (4, 5) . Patient s responsive to IC I commonly harbor an immune -infiltrated (hot) tumor 58 

microenvironment (TME ), characterized by pre -existing anti -tumor immunity and abundant immune cell 59 

infiltration. However, the remaining 75% - 90% of patients do not respond owing to the immun osuppressive 60 

properties (cold) of TME characterized by limited immune cell infiltration into the TME (6 -8) . Consequently, 61 

developing safe and efficacious strategies to reprogram the immunosuppressive TME and enhance anti -tumor 62 

immune response s remains a critical unmet medical need. 63 

MG53 (also known as TRIM72 ) is a member of the tripartite motif (TRIM) protein family that was originally 64 

characterized as a key molecule in cell membrane repair (9) . Our group and others have extensively 65 

demonstrated that recombinant human MG53 (rhMG53) protein exert s broad therapeutic activity across multiple 66 

organ systems, including muscular dystrophy (9, 10) , lung injury (11, 12) , myocardial infarction (13) , kidney 67 

injury (14, 15) , and wound healing (16, 17) . Beyond its membrane repair function, accumulating evidence has 68 

established MG53 as a critical regulator of cellular homeostasis by modulating oxidative stress, autophagy, and 69 

inflammation (18, 19) . Importantly, w e recently uncovered an antitumor function of MG53 in lung cancer (20) . We70 

also demonstrated that rhMG53 acts synergistically with chemotherapeutic agents to promote death of lung 71 

cancer cells and suppress tumor growth of drug -resistant cancers in colorectal carcinoma (21) . Several 72 

subsequent studies further support the function for MG53 as an antitumor therapeutic agent (22 -24) . The dual 73 

properties —tumor suppression and immune modulation —make MG53 an attractive candidate payload for 74 

arming an oncolytic virus, potentially coupling enhanced cytotoxic with a more immunogenic mode of tumor cell 75 

death. 76

Oncolytic virotherapy (OV) has emerged as a safe and promising platform in cancer immunotherapy ,77 

distinguished by its capacity to selectively lyse tumor cells while spar ing normal cells , and to simultaneously 78 

reprogram the antitumor immune response state (25 -27) . The T -Vec (Talimogene laherparepvec) is an attenuated 79 

herpes simplex virus ty pe 1 (HSV -1) engineered to encode granulocyte -macrophage colony -stimulating factor 80 

(GM -CSF) . It received FDA approv al in the USA for intratumoral treatment of advanced melanoma and 81 

demonstrated synergistic antitumor efficacy when combined with immune checkpoint inhibitors (28) . Measles 82 

virus ( MeV) is a paramyxovirus and another attractive oncolytic virus. Using reverse genetics system, MeV can 83 

be engineered to express therapeutic transgene s insertion (27) . MeV exhibits inherent tumor tropism mediated 84 

by the overexpression of CD46, a complement regulatory protein uniformly upregulated on human tumor cells 85 

relative to normal tissues (29, 30) . Attenuated vaccine strain of MeV is an attractive oncolytic backbone due to its 86 

established clinical safety record, natural tropism for tumor cells overexpressing the viral entry receptor CD46, 87 

and capacity to induce immunogenic cell death . Critically , engineering MeV to express therapeutic transgenes —88 

a strategy already validated for cytokines, suicide genes, and immune -stimulatory payloads —offers a modular 89 

approach to enhance oncolytic potency and reshape the tumor immune microenvironment . However, the optimal 90 

transgene cargo for maximizing both direct cytotoxicity and immunogenicity in NSCLC remains an active area of 91 

investigation. 92 

A mechanistica lly critical modality by which oncolytic viruses potentiate antitumor immunity is through the 93 

induction of Gasdermin -mediated pyroptosis (31) . Unlike classical apoptosis, which is generally considered 94 

immunologically silent, pyroptosis is a lytic, inflammatory form of cell death characterized by membrane pore 95 

formation, releas e of damage -associated molecular patterns (DAMPs), and robust recruitment of immune 96 

effector cells (32, 33) . GSDME, a key member of the Gasdermin protein family, is specifically cleaved and 97 

activated by caspase -3, thereby converting conventional apoptotic signaling into immunogenic pyroptotic cell 98 

death (32) . GSDME -mediated pyroptosis has been demonstrated to profoundly reshape the TME, converting 99 

immunologically "cold" tumors into "hot," immune -infiltrated states and potentiating the efficacy of immune 100 

checkpoint blockade across multiple cancer models (33) . The caspase -3/GSDME pyroptosis axis thus represents 101 

an attractive target for enhancing antitumor immunotherapeutic efficacy .102

therapeutic potential against NSCLC . We hypothesized that MG53 arming would not only preserve the oncolytic 104

Discussion 462 

In this study, we demonstrate that engineering oncolytic MeV with the tumor suppressor MG53 (rMeV -MG53) 463 

enhances both direct cytotoxicity and antitumor immunity in NSCLC, converging on activation of the caspase -464 

3/GSDME pyroptotic axis. rMeV -MG53 preserved the replication competence of the parental MeV vector while 465 

significantly exceeding an unarmed control in killing NSCLC cells, amplified pro -inflammatory cytokine signaling 466 

both intrinsically and during infection, drove robust effector T -cell infiltration in vivo , and sensitized tumors to PD -467 

L1 blockade. Together, these findings establish MG53 as a mechanistically distinct and therapeutically attractive 468 

payload for oncolytic virus engineering for the treatment of lung cancer. 469 

The attenuated MeV Edmonston vaccine strain has been extensively investigated as an oncolytic platform across 470 

multiple cancer types, with favorable safety profiles documented in early -phase clinical trials (25, 29, 30, 40) . The 471 

tractable MeV reverse genetics system enables insertion of therapeutic transgenes as additional transcription 472 

units, a strategy that has been exploited to arm MeV with a range of immunomodulatory payloads including GM -473 

CSF, IL -12, anti -PD -L1 antibody fragments, and sodium iodide symporter (25, 30, 40) . To our knowledge, this is 474 

the first study to employ MG53 as a therapeutic transgene in an oncolytic virus platform, thereby expanding the 475 

repertoire of therapeutic payloads available for armed oncolytic virotherapy. MeV infects cells using 476 

CD150/SLAM, nectin -4, and the complement regulator CD46, the last of which is commonly overexpressed on 477 

the surface of tumor cells as a mechanism to evade complement -mediated lysis (29, 41) . However, the murine 478 

orthologs of these receptors do not support efficient MeV entry, precluding viral replication in murine tumor cells. 479 

To circumvent receptor restriction barriers, we engineered murine cells with stable expression of hCD46, 480 

establishing a syngeneic, MeV -permissive model that —unlike immunodeficient xenografts —permits 481 

comprehensive assessment of virus -induced immune responses and their interaction with checkpoint blockade 482 

in an immunocompetent host. Importantly, the viral delivery strategy employed here directly addresses a critical 483 

pharmacokinetic limitation of systemically administered recombinant MG53 protein, which exhibits a circulating 484 

half -life of approximately 1.5 h in preclinical models (10, 14) . By encoding MG53 within the replicating viral 485 

genome, rMeV -MG53 achieves continuous, tumor -localized MG53 production throughout the course of viral 486 

replication, enabling sustained intratumoral MG53 exposure that is fundamentally unattainable through 487

Several limitations of this study merit mention: First, as MeV is a primate -adapted pathogen with limited 550 

replication in immunocompetent rodents, the LL/2 syngeneic model used here does not fully recapitulate human 551 

MeV replication kinetics and spread. This is a well -recognized challenge in the preclinical development of 552 

oncolytic MeV and argues for future validation in hCD46 -transgenic mouse models or non -human primate 553 

systems that better approximate MeV biology in humans (48) . Alternatively, a comparative medicine approach 554 

using canine pulmonary adenocarcinoma could be considered. Canine pulmonary adenocarcinoma has been 555 

proposed as a model for NSCLC (49) . Studies have also demonstrated that MeV lacking its binding affinity for 556 

SLAM has an antitumor effect on canine lung cancer cell line (50) . Translation of our work to canine pulmonary 557 

adenocarcinoma may facilitate investigation in an immunocompetent spontaneous disease model that may more 558

Based on our collective findings, we propose the following working model ( Figure 6D ). Upon intratumoral delivery, 572 

rMeV -MG53 infects NSCLC cells via hCD46 or other related receptors, achieving sustained MG53 expression 573 

through viral replication. Virus -delivered MG53 amplifies caspase -3 cleavage, triggering dual apoptosis and 574 

GSDME -mediated pyroptosis, driving release of TNF -α, IL -6, IL -1β, and IFN -β and converting an 575 

immunologically “cold” TME into an immune -infiltrated “hot” TME. CXCL10 induction recruits CD8 ⁺ effector T 576 

cells, which exert cytotoxicity via Granzyme B and IFN -γ. However, this immune activation simultaneously 577 

induces compensatory PD -L1 upregulation on tumor cells, establishing an adaptive resistance checkpoint that 578 

constrains single -agent virotherapy efficacy. Combining rMeV -MG53 with anti -PD -L1 blockade relieves this 579 

checkpoint, fully unleashing infiltrating CD8 ⁺ T-cell cytotoxicity and achieving the strongest antitumor control 580 

observed across all treatment conditions. This working model positions rMeV -MG53 + anti -PD -L1 as a 581 

mechanistically rational combination strategy to sensitize immune -excluded NSCLC to checkpoint 582 

immunotherapy. 583 

In conclusion, this study establishes MG53 as a rationally designed payload for oncolytic MeV that 584 

simultaneously enhances direct tumor cytotoxicity through GSDME -mediated pyroptosis and remodels the tumor 585 

immune microenvironment to favor effector T -cell infiltration. By coupling this dual mechanism with PD -L1 586 

blockade, rMeV -MG53 achieved superior tumor control in an immunocompetent NSCLC model, offering a 587 

promising combinatorial strategy to sensitize immunologically cold or checkpoint -resistant lung tumors to 588 

immunotherapy. These findings provide a strong mechanistic and preclinical foundation for the further 589 

development of rMeV -MG53 as a novel therapeutic strategy for lung cancer. 590
