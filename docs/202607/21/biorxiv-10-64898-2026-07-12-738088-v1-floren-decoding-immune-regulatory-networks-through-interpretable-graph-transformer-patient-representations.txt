Summary 14 

> 15

Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) enables detailed characterization of cellular heterogeneity, 16 

yet understanding the full cellular and regulatory environment of complex tissues remains 17 

challenging. In the era of large single-cell atlases, this technology has become increasingly 18 

accessible, and datasets have grown in scale and statistical power. As a result, sample 19 

representation methods have emerged as a promising strategy to summarize patient-level 20 

biological variation. However, most existing approaches rely on unsupervised learning frameworks 21 

with ambiguous biological interpretability. 22 

> 23

Here we present a Framework for Learning Over REgulatory-Embedding Networks (FloREN), a 24 

supervised and interpretable sample representation method. FloREN models single-cell data as a 25 

heterogeneous network integrating cells and genes together with gene regulatory and cell-cell 26 

communication relationships. Through condition-aware embeddings and interpretable attention 27 

networks, FloREN enables improved sample stratification and biomarker discovery. In addition, the 28 

framework supports downstream analyses that found specific immune network mechanisms in 29 

immune-mediated inflammatory diseases (IMIDs). 30 

> 31
> 32

Introduction 45 

> 46

Advances in high-throughput technologies over the past decade have enabled the generation of 47 

increasingly large and complex biological datasets, transforming our ability to study human disease 48 

at unprecedented resolution 1-4 . Among these technologies, single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) 49 

has emerged as a cornerstone for dissecting cellular heterogeneity. Profiling hundreds of millions of 50 

cells across diverse tissues and disease contexts has revealed novel cell types 5, context-specific 51 

marker genes 6,7 , gene programs linked to therapeutic response, and clinically relevant patient 52 

subpopulations 8,9 . As scRNA-seq studies expand toward cohort-scale analyses, methods that 53 

summarize each sample as a single vector, commonly termed sample representation methods, have 54 

become increasingly important. These approaches capture coordinated changes across tissue 55 

ecosystems and enable direct linkage between sample-level phenotypes and their underlying 56 

cellular and molecular composition 10,11 .57 

> 58

Existing methods can be broadly divided into unsupervised and supervised approaches. 59 

Unsupervised methods aim to capture global similarities between samples without relying on 60 

labeled outcomes, and are effective for sample stratification, batch correction, and exploratory 61 

analysis. Supervised methods instead learn embeddings optimized for downstream tasks such as 62 

disease classification or outcome prediction, where interpretability becomes essential for 63 

translating learned representations into candidate biomarkers or drug targets. 64 

> 65

The simplest strategy is pseudobulk aggregation, which averages gene expression across cells within 66 

each sample. Although pseudobulk profiles retain substantial biological information and support 67 

accurate patient classification 12 , they are difficult to relate back to cell-type-specific or single-cell-68 

resolved patterns. Compositional approaches instead represent samples through the distribution of 69 

cell states: GloScope 13 models each sample as a distribution over cell annotations using Gaussian 70 

mixture models (GMMs) on PCA embeddings in an unsupervised setting, while CloudPred 14 trains a 71 

supervised classifier on similar GMM-based representations; both, however, remain difficult to 72 

interpret at the level of individual genes or pathways. An alternative strategy represents each 73 

sample using a broad panel of precomputed single-cell statistics (differential abundance (DA), 74 

differential gene expression (DGE), gene set enrichment analysis (GSEA), and cell-cell 75 

communication scores) as implemented in scFeatures 15 . More recently, the machine learning 76 

community has developed foundation models for single-cell cohorts, including PaSCient 11 and 77 

PULSAR 17 , which aim to disentangle biological from technical variation in an unsupervised setting, 78 

and mcBERT 16 , which leverages large-scale pretraining to improve supervised predictive 79 

performance; several of these incorporate learned attention weights in an effort to improve 80 

interpretability of cell- and gene-level contributions. SampleCLR 18 combines contrastive self-81 

supervised and supervised objectives over PCA embeddings and outperforms earlier foundation 82 

models showing that the potential of this techniques, though its interpretability remains limited to 83 

cell-level weights. MrVI 10 , which relies on an unsupervised variational inference framework, extends 84 

interpretability further by linking sample representations to DGE and DA analyses. Collectively, 85 

these approaches reveal a persistent trade-off: no existing method combines a clearly supervised 86 

objective with interpretability across multiple biological levels: cells, genes, and their interactions. 87 

> 88

To address this gap, we introduce FloREN (Framework for Learning Over REgulatory-Embedding 89 

Networks), a graph neural network (GNN)-based method that models sample-level single-cell data 90 

as biological networks and leverages attention-based interpretability to advance the state of the art 91 

in sample representation learning. 92

Biological systems are inherently networked, with genes, proteins, and cells interacting in 94 

coordinated ways; these relationships can be naturally represented as graphs, in which nodes 95 

denote biological entities and edges encode their interactions 19,20 . As datasets grow in scale and 96 

complexity, graph-based modeling has emerged as a powerful framework for integrating and 97 

analyzing high-dimensional biological data 20 . Accordingly, GNNs have been widely adopted in single-98 

cell analysis, enabling applications such as data imputation 21–25 , clustering 26–32 , and cell-type 99 

annotation 33–38 . Despite this progress, most GNN-based approaches rely on homogeneous graphs 100 

that typically represent only cells and their transcriptomic similarity 39 , overlooking the inherent 101 

heterogeneity of biological systems, a limitation that is especially consequential for sample-level 102 

inference. Only a handful of methods move beyond this paradigm: DeepMAPS 40 and Segger 41 

> 103

incorporate heterogeneous graphs but are designed for clustering and segmentation tasks, 104 

respectively, while Cellograph 42 and OmicsGAT 43 use more limited graph structures for differential 105 

expression or outcome prediction. There thus remains a clear need for models that fully exploit 106 

biological heterogeneity to generate interpretable, patient-level representations. 107 

> 108

This challenge is especially relevant to immune-mediated inflammatory diseases (IMIDs), where 109 

pathology arises from system-wide dysregulation of the immune response 44 . These diseases, 110 

including rheumatoid arthritis (RA), systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), and multiple sclerosis (MS), 111 

involve complex interactions among diverse immune cell populations, and their pathology reflects 112 

coordinated network-level alterations rather than isolated cellular changes, making IMIDs a 113 

stringent benchmark for evaluating sample representation methods 45 .114 

> 115

That’s why we propose FloREN, an interpretable sample representation framework that models 116 

single-cell data as heterogeneous biological networks. From SampleCLR contrastive self-supervised 117 

and supervised promising combination, FloREN combines heterogeneous graph transformers, graph 118 

self-supervised learning, and supervised optimization to generate biologically informed patient 119 

embeddings while preserving interpretability through attention-based prioritization of cells, genes, 120 

and regulatory interactions. By jointly integrating cellular composition, gene regulation, and 121 

intercellular communication within a unified graph representation, FloREN enables both accurate 122 

patient stratification and mechanistic discovery from single-cell datasets. 123

Results 143 

The architecture of FloREN. 144 

> 145

FloREN is a graph neural network (GNN) framework designed to extract biologically meaningful 146 

patterns from scRNA-seq data by jointly modeling multi-layered relationships within heterogeneous 147 

graphs, gene expression, regulatory interactions, and intercellular communication ( Fig. 1a ). FloREN 148 

takes as input a single-cell expression object with log-normalized counts, restricted to a defined 149 

gene set for the analysis (typically highly variable genes, HVGs). Beyond gene expression, FloREN 150 

can also operate directly on transcription factor (TF) activity scores derived from tools such as 151 

DecoupleR 63 , allowing the framework to model regulatory state rather than expression alone when 152 

desired. FloREN first projects cells and genes into a shared latent space using paired autoencoders. 153 

This denoising step generates comparable low-dimensional representations that enable robust 154 

inference of heterogeneous biological interactions while reducing the impact of sparse single-cell 155 

measurements. 156 

> 157

The core of FloREN is to use this same dimensionality space to construct a heterogeneous graph for 158 

each sample, in which nodes correspond to cells and genes (or TFs), and node features correspond 159 

to their learned embeddings. Edges are defined across three biologically motivated interaction 160 

layers. First, gene-cell edges link each gene to the cells expressing it. Second, gene-gene edges 161 

capture regulatory relationships inferred through a Bayesian scoring strategy (see Methods ) that 162 

integrates curated immune interaction databases with correlation estimates, enabling detection of 163 

both known and novel regulatory associations. Third, optional cell-cell communication edges can be 164 

incorporated using curated ligand-receptor interaction networks, for example via CellChat 66 . This 165 

modular design gives FloREN substantial flexibility in graph construction, allowing users to include 166 

or omit interaction layers depending on data availability and biological question. 167 

> 168

These heterogeneous graphs are processed using our Heterogeneous Graph Transformer 169 

Contrastive Learning (HGTCL) architecture ( Fig. 1b ) which combines the Heterogeneous Graph 170 

Transformer (HGT) 71 with graph Contrastive Learning (graphCL) 67 . Because biological graphs derived 171 

from single-cell data are inherently noisy and sensitive to graph construction, HGTCL first learns 172 

robust sample representations in a self-supervised manner by maximizing agreement between two 173 

stochastic augmentations of the same graph generated through node dropping. Within each HGT 174 

layer, relation-specific attention propagates information across cells, genes, and biological 175 

interactions, enabling context-aware integration of heterogeneous signals while remaining invariant 176 

to minor perturbations of the graph. Following the HGT layers, node embeddings are aggregated by 177 

mean pooling into a single fixed-dimensional sample representation. Although contrastive learning 178 

produces robust graph representations, it does not ensure that they capture disease-relevant 179 

variation. FloREN therefore introduces a supervised multilayer perceptron (MLP) operating on the 180 

learned sample embeddings. This objective steer representation learning toward phenotype-181 

associated biological differences while preserving the structural information captured during self-182 

supervised training, enabling both multitask and multiclass prediction (see Methods )46,47 .183 

> 184

Together, these components allow FloREN to produce, from a single training pass, three 185 

complementary outputs: supervised sample-level embeddings from the MLP classifier, condition-186 

aware cell, and gene embeddings from the HGT encoder, and an interpretable attention network 187 

describing cell-, gene-, and interaction-level contributions to each sample's representation, enabling 188 

a range of downstream analyses discussed below. 189

FloREN demonstrates superior performance across diverse single-cell studies. 240 

> 241

To evaluate FloREN's generalizability, we applied it to five independent IMIDs scRNA-seq datasets 242 

spanning diverse immunological contexts, experimental designs, and patient cohorts ( Fig. 2a ; see 243 

Methods )48-52 . For each dataset, we constructed patient-level heterogeneous graphs reflecting the 244 

specific structure of that study; together, these five scenarios also serve as an ablation test of 245 

FloREN's performance across varying graph compositions and data regimes. 246 

> 247

We benchmarked FloREN against a panel of leading, widely used methods spanning the main 248 

strategies described above. As baselines for gene-expression information retention, we used 249 

sample-level pseudobulk profiles and a k-nearest-neighbors (kNN) classifier applied to pseudobulk 250 

embeddings. As baselines for cell-composition information retention, we used GloScope, which 251 

models a Gaussian mixture model (GMM) over cells' PCA embeddings in an unsupervised setting, 252 

and its supervised counterpart CloudPred, which fits a patient classifier using the same GMM-based 253 

representation. We additionally included scFeatures, an algorithmic approach that computes a 254 

comprehensive panel of single-cell summary statistics per patient, to assess whether learned 255 

representations capture information beyond what these hand-crafted statistics already provide. We 256 

further benchmarked against both unsupervised and supervised variants of SampleCLR; making us 257 

exclude PULSAR, PaSCient, or mcBERT, as these were benchmarked and proved substantially more 258 

difficult to adapt to our datasets. Finally, we included MrVI as a representative of variational-259 

inference-based single-cell (scVI-family) approaches, an increasingly prominent strategy in this field. 260 

All baselines were compared against two FloREN outputs: the self-supervised contrastive 261 

embeddings, and the final layer of the supervised FloREN classifier. 262 

> 263

Across all five datasets, FloREN embeddings outperformed alternative sample-level representation 264 

methods according to the Sample Preservation and Representation Evaluation (SPARE) metrics 53 

> 265

(see Methods ). FloREN achieved the highest overall SPARE score ( Fig. 2b ), driven primarily by 266 

superior disease-information retention, while remaining competitive in batch-effect removal, age-267 

trajectory conservation, and robustness to sex. Self-supervised FloREN embeddings performed 268 

comparably to other unsupervised methods, including unsupervised SampleCLR, scFeatures, and 269 

GloScope, but fell behind MrVI in this setting ( Fig. 2c ). In contrast, the supervised FloREN classifier's 270 

final-layer embeddings consistently outperformed all supervised and unsupervised baselines across 271 

SPARE metrics. The FloREN classifier's advantage was most pronounced for disease-information 272 

retention, where it obtained the highest scores across unsupervised clustering metrics (Average 273 

Silhouette Width [ASW], inverse Davies-Bouldin score [−DBS], and Calinski-Harabasz index [CH]), 274 

supervised clustering metrics (Adjusted Mutual Information [AMI] and Adjusted Rand Index [ARI]), 275 

and predictive-performance metrics (5-fold cross-validated F1 score, area under the receiver 276 

operating characteristic curve [AUC], and balanced accuracy) ( Fig. 2d ). For batch- and sex-277 

information removal, FloREN's performance was comparable to the top baselines, with the kNN 278 

classifier on pseudobulk embeddings and CloudPred achieving the best scores on these specific 279 

tasks ( Fig. 2e,f ). The combined evaluation of supervised and unsupervised disease metrics, together 280 

with the agreement between SPARE and AUC scores, confirms the robustness of these results ( Fig. 281 

2g ). 282 

> 283

Finally, in terms of computational performance, FloREN required longer runtimes than PCA-based 284 

methods such as SampleCLR but was comparable to more computationally demanding approaches 285 

such as GloScope (GMM fitting) and MrVI (variational inference over the full count matrix) ( Fig. 2h ). 286 

FloREN's memory usage was higher than that of SampleCLR or MrVI, but remained comparable to 287 

scFeatures, which computes thousands of statistics per patient ( Fig. 2i ). 288

FloREN generates patient-level representations that reveal disease trajectories and intermediate 386 

SLE states. 387 

> 388

We used the Perez dataset 52 , originally designed to train supervised models distinguishing SLE 389 

patients from healthy donors, to evaluate whether supervised and unsupervised sample 390 

representation methods could additionally distinguish SLE patients experiencing disease flares 391 

(FLARE) from non-flaring SLE patients, a substantially higher disease-activity state. 392 

> 393

We found this to be a considerably harder task: no clear separation between flare and non-flare 394 

status emerged in UMAP, t-SNE, PCA, or PHATE visualizations for any method, with the exception 395 

of GloScope, which we instead visualized using UMAP, MDS, Isomap, and spectral embedding, 396 

reflecting its distributional (GMM-based) representation. Despite the absence of clear visual 397 

separation, FloREN's self-supervised embeddings ranked among the top-performing methods, 398 

alongside pseudobulk, the kNN classifier on pseudobulk embeddings, MrVI, and both unsupervised 399 

and supervised SampleCLR, based on 5-fold cross-validated F1, AUC, and balanced accuracy scores 400 

(Fig. 3a ). 401 

> 402

Hierarchical clustering based on Euclidean distance between condition-mean cell embeddings 403 

showed that SLE and healthy donor (HD) samples were related but not fully aligned, with distinct 404 

cellular niches emerging within each condition ( Fig. 3b ). Plasmablasts and progenitor cells showed 405 

clear separation between SLE and HD samples, consistent with their high importance in the 406 

attention-based analysis. Within-condition clustering further identified a distinct activation substate 407 

specific to these two populations, alongside a broadly connected cluster comprising CD4+ and CD8+ 408 

T cells, classical and non-classical monocytes (cM/ncM), natural killer (NK) cells, and B cells. 409 

Proliferative cells, conventional dendritic cells (cDCs), and plasmacytoid dendritic cells (pDCs) 410 

formed a shared niche in HD samples, whereas pDCs separated from this niche in SLE patients, 411 

consistent with their established role as the primary source of type I interferon in autoimmune 412 

disease 54 .413 

> 414

To further assess whether FloREN embeddings capture continuous disease progression, we 415 

analyzed the Nehar-Belaid dataset 51 , in which patients are annotated with discrete SLE disease 416 

stages. FloREN distinguished SLE patients from healthy donors with high accuracy (F1 = 0.85, ROC-417 

AUC = 0.80), and the position of intermediate-stage patients within the embedding space tracked 418 

with their annotated disease progression ( Fig. 3c ). A lower-dimensional projection of the FloREN 419 

embedding space further separated SLE patients from HDs ( Fig. 3d ) and revealed three distinct 420 

subgroups: SLE-control-like, canonical SLE, and SLE-aggressive. Trajectory inference using 421 

Slingshot 55 recapitulated a continuous progression from HDs through these intermediate states to 422 

SLE-aggressive. This stratification was independent of batch, age, and sex effects. 423

FloREN identifies disease-relevant cellular populations and gene signatures in multiple sclerosis. 532 

> 533

We next applied FloREN in a top-down analytical setting, using it as a first-line strategy for 534 

identifying key cellular and molecular drivers of disease directly from patient cohorts. We analyzed 535 

the Straeten dataset 49 , an integrated CSF and PBMC scRNA-seq compendium comprising 17 multiple 536 

sclerosis (MS) patients and 11 healthy donors (HD). The cell-type annotation performed by the 537 

original authors procedure 49,56 yielded cell-type predictions at two levels of granularity: Level 1 538 

(major cell types) and Level 2 (fine-grained subtypes). 539 

> 540

Using the same dimensionality-reduction panel applied to the Perez dataset, we first confirmed that 541 

FloREN achieves the highest separation of MS patients from HDs among all benchmarked methods 542 

(Fig. 4a ) and that this separation emerges progressively over the course of classifier training. We 543 

then computed the mean attention score for each cell type across patients to prioritize populations 544 

for downstream analysis. Monocytes emerged as the most influential cell type at Level 1 ( Fig. 4b ). 545 

At Level 2, CD14⁺ and CD16⁺ monocytes remained among the top-ranked populations, while 546 

AXL⁺SIGLEC6⁺ dendritic cells (ASDCs) showed the strongest attention signal. ASDCs represent a 547 

transitional population between plasmacytoid and conventional dendritic cells with strong T-cell-548 

activating and migratory capacity 57,58 ; their prominence in our analysis suggests a previously 549 

underappreciated role in shaping the neuroinflammatory environment in MS. FloREN also assigned 550 

high attention to several poorly defined clusters ("Un_assigned," "other," and "other_T"), 551 

suggesting that their original annotation may warrant reevaluation. Consistent with the attention-552 

based prioritization, the relevance of key CD4⁺ subtypes was independently confirmed through 553 

UMAP visualization ( Fig. 4d ). 554 

> 555

We then examined gene-level attention within each cell type ( Fig. 4c ), which revealed a shared 556 

inflammatory signature spanning multiple immune populations: complement activation 557 

(C1QA/B/C), citrullination (PADI2), type I interferon signaling (ISG15, IFI6), costimulatory receptors 558 

(TNFRSF4, TNFRSF9, TNFRSF18), and tissue-residency factors (ZNF683, RUNX3). The prioritization of 559 

CHI3L2, a known MS biomarker 59 , further supports the biological relevance of FloREN's attention-560 

based rankings. To resolve cell-type-specific programs within monocytes, the most influential 561 

population identified above, we applied gene program decomposition (see Methods ), uncovering a 562 

distinct monocyte-enriched program ( Fig. 4e ) centered on IL10 as a hub gene. Notably, this program 563 

also included markers typically associated with lymphoid lineages, such as ZAP70, FCRL5, and 564 

BANK1. We interpret this as more consistent with a hybrid activation state or strong intercellular 565 

communication signaling between myeloid and lymphoid compartments than with simple 566 

misannotation 49 , though we cannot fully exclude residual annotation noise. Additionally, the 567 

prominence of SATB1, A1CF, HLA-DRB1, and S100A12 suggests a broader molecular reprogramming 568 

of the myeloid lineage in MS. 569 

> 570

Finally, we performed a differential abundance (DA) analysis using FloREN's attention scores. 571 

Because FloREN assigns an attention score to each cell, cumulative attention per cell type can be 572 

computed for each patient; under the assumption of comparable per-cell attention contributions 573 

across conditions, a higher cumulative attention score for a given cell type reflects its increased 574 

relative representation in that condition. Using this approach, we observed increased attention-575 

based representation of CD4⁺ T cells, CD8⁺ T cells, B cells, and NK cells in MS relative to HD ( Fig. 4f ). 576 

These findings agree with MrVI's DA analysis for B cells, dendritic cells, and monocytes, but diverge 577 

for NK, CD4⁺, and CD8⁺ T cells ( Fig. 4f ). 578

FloREN reveals regulatory gene programs and immune cell drivers of rheumatoid arthritis. 678 

> 679

We next applied FloREN as a bottom-up analytical framework, in contrast to the top-down approach 680 

used for MS, to the Binvignat dataset 48 , comprising PBMC samples from 18 rheumatoid arthritis (RA) 681 

patients and 18 matched healthy controls (HC). To capture intercellular interactions in this analysis, 682 

we incorporated CellChat-derived communication edges into the FloREN graph. 683 

> 684

We confirmed that FloREN achieves the highest separation of RA patients from HCs among all 685 

benchmarked methods ( Fig. 5a ), with this separation emerging progressively over the course of 686 

classifier training. Attention-based ranking of cell populations identified plasmablasts, γδ T cells, 687 

effector memory CD4⁺ αβ T cells, NK cells, and central memory CD4⁺ αβ T cells as the most 688 

informative populations ( Fig. 5b ). Analysis of attention-weighted cell-cell communication further 689 

revealed a global reduction in intercellular signaling in RA relative to HC (see Methods ). From these 690 

top-ranked cell types, we derived gene-attention scores and their associated signatures ( Fig. 5c ), 691 

capturing key RA-relevant mechanisms including citrullination (PADI2/4), type I interferon signaling 692 

(ISG15, IFI6), and metabolic regulation (SLC2A1, PGD). To further characterize disease-associated 693 

genes, we compared gene embeddings between conditions. Aggregating gene embeddings by 694 

condition enabled a differential analysis that identified 99 genes significantly separating RA from HC 695 

(FDR<0.05) ( Fig. 5d ). These genes formed a metabolic and structural effector module enriched for 696 

mitochondrial transcripts (MT-CO1/2/3, MT-ATP6) and matrix-remodeling components (COL6A2, 697 

PACSIN2). While this module partially overlapped with attention-derived signatures, it primarily 698 

reflected downstream effector processes rather than upstream regulatory activity. By contrast, 699 

FloREN's attention scores highlighted regulatory hubs integrating citrullination (PADI2/4), interferon 700 

signaling (ISG15, IFI6), and complement initiation (C1QA), underscoring the complementary 701 

information captured by differential expression versus model-derived attention. 702 

> 703

To place these findings in a functional context, we performed community detection on the 704 

combined set of top attention-ranked and differentially expressed genes. This identified nine gene 705 

programs, annotated via Fisher's exact test (see Methods ), representing coordinated immune states 706 

including interferon signaling, inflammatory monocyte priming, metabolic reprogramming, and 707 

complement activation. Notably, we observed condition-associated shifts in antigen-processing and 708 

B-cell activation programs. Increased activity of the B-cell activation program mapped specifically 709 

to memory B cells, naive B cells, and plasmablasts, refining the cell populations implicated by our 710 

earlier attention-based ranking ( Fig. 5e ). The co-regulation of FCRL1 with metabolic (GLUL) and cell-711 

cycle (G0S2) genes further suggests a coordinated, system-wide activation state extending beyond 712 

the B-cell compartment alone. 713 

> 714

By prioritizing key cell populations, identifying disease-relevant genes, and organizing them into 715 

coherent regulatory programs, FloREN reveals a coordinated immune network in RA ( Fig. 5f ) in 716 

which citrullination and interferon signaling act as central hubs orchestrating systemic 717 

inflammation, advancing the identification of network-level biomarkers beyond single-cell-type 718 

markers alone. 719

Discussion 825 

> 826

Single-cell RNA sequencing has transformed our understanding of cellular heterogeneity in human 827 

disease. Standard analyses typically characterize differences at the level of individual cell types 828 

between conditions, relying on expert interpretation to integrate these findings into a coherent, 829 

system-level view of the cellular environment. As single-cell atlases have grown in scale, sample 830 

representation methods have emerged as a promising strategy for summarizing this complexity into 831 

patient-level signatures. Yet even in the era of foundation models, most such methods remain either 832 

unsupervised, offering no direct link to phenotypes of interest, or supervised without 833 

interpretability, leaving the biological basis of their predictions opaque. This trade-off has confined 834 

sample representation largely to exploratory analysis, short of its potential as a tool for hypothesis-835 

driven discovery. 836 

> 837

FloREN (Framework for Learning Over REgulatory-Embedding Networks) addresses this gap directly. 838 

Rather than treating supervision and interpretability as competing design goals, FloREN unifies them 839 

within a single graph-based architecture: single-cell data is modeled as a heterogeneous biological 840 

network of cells and genes linked through regulatory and communicative relationships, and a 841 

combination of self-supervised and supervised objectives learns condition-relevant sample 842 

embeddings through this network via the HGTCL architecture. Because attention is computed 843 

natively over this biological graph, every prediction FloREN makes is traceable back to the specific 844 

cells, genes, and interactions that support it, turning sample representation from a black-box 845 

summarization step into a transparent, task-driven analysis in its own right. 846 

> 847

Across five independent immunological datasets spanning RA, MS, and SLE, FloREN consistently 848 

outperformed existing sample representation approaches while preserving this interpretability at 849 

every stage of the pipeline. The framework yields three complementary outputs from a single 850 

model: supervised sample-level embeddings optimized for classification, condition-aware cell and 851 

gene embeddings that capture disease-relevant structure, and an attention network that assigns 852 

interpretable scores to nodes and interactions across the biological graph. In SLE, these sample 853 

embeddings captured not only overall disease status but also continuous disease trajectories, 854 

resolving intermediate and aggressive states consistent with independent pseudotime inference. 855 

FloREN's cell embeddings further revealed condition-specific cellular niches, including a shift in 856 

plasmacytoid dendritic cell association consistent with their established role in type I interferon-857 

driven autoimmunity. In MS, attention-based differential abundance analysis highlighted a distinct 858 

inflammatory and reprogramming program within monocytes, converging on regulatory hubs, 859 

including interferon-stimulated genes and complement components, that mirrored mechanisms 860 

independently identified in SLE and RA. In RA, gene embeddings enabled differential expression 861 

analysis alongside attention-based prioritization of a B-cell activation program centered on 862 

plasmablasts, which emerged as a core hub within a broader systemic inflammation network. 863 

Notably, the recurrence of type I interferon and complement-related signatures across all three, 864 

biologically and etiologically distinct diseases suggests that FloREN is not merely fitting dataset-865 

specific noise but recovering a shared regulatory architecture underlying immune-mediated 866 

inflammation, precisely the kind of network-level convergence that motivated our use of IMIDs as 867 

a stringent evaluation setting. 868 

> 869

These results illustrate the central contribution of this work: FloREN demonstrates that supervision 870 

and interpretability need not be in tension in sample representation learning. Where prior 871 

unsupervised methods offer interpretability without a clear predictive objective, and prior 872 

supervised methods offer strong predictive performance with limited mechanistic insight, FloREN's 873 

> .CC-BY-NC-ND 4.0 International license made available under a (which was not certified by peer review) is the author/funder, who has granted bioRxiv a license to display the preprint in perpetuity. It is The copyright holder for this preprint this version posted July 20, 2026. ;https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.07.12.738088 doi: bioRxiv preprint

graph-native attention mechanism ties both together. Every embedding dimension and every 874 

attention score is grounded in an explicit biological entity or interaction, rather than an abstract 875 

latent coordinate. In this sense, FloREN aims to mark a step forward for sample representation 876 

methods: moving the field toward approaches that combine a clearly defined, task-relevant learning 877 

objective with biologically interpretable network modeling, rather than requiring a trade-off 878 

between the two. 879 

> 880

Despite its strengths, several limitations should be considered. First, FloREN’s computational 881 

complexity scales with both the number of samples and the density of graph edges, making training 882 

on large-scale datasets resource intensive. Future work could mitigate this through graph 883 

sparsification, subgraph sampling, or more efficient mini-batch training strategies for 884 

heterogeneous graphs. Incorporating pretrained or foundation models to initialize cell and gene 885 

embeddings represents a promising direction to further improve performance and scalability. 886 

Second, the framework might rely on curated prior knowledge (e.g., gene-gene interactions and 887 

cell-cell communication databases) and preprocessing choices such as gene selection and cell type 888 

annotation. Variability or bias in these inputs may influence downstream representations and 889 

interpretability. Applications outside the immune system will require new prior knowledge 890 

generation if it wants to be used. Third, while attention scores highlight statistical importance within 891 

the model, they do not by themselves establish causal or mechanistic roles; complementary 892 

perturbation-based validation will be necessary to confirm the functional relevance of prioritized 893 

genes and populations, such as CHI3L2 in MS or the FCRL1-centered program in RA. Finally, our 894 

evaluation focused on IMIDs, chosen for their network-level pathology; extending FloREN to other 895 

disease contexts, including solid tumors and neurodegenerative disease, will be important to 896 

establish the generality of this approach. 897 

> 898

Looking forward, FloREN's modular graph construction makes it well suited to increasingly complex 899 

experimental designs. Extensions to longitudinal cohorts could enable direct modeling of temporal 900 

disease dynamics rather than the cross-sectional trajectory inference used here, while integration 901 

with spatial transcriptomics or additional omics layers may yield richer, tissue-resolved 902 

representations of cellular organization and regulation. 903 

> 904

In summary, FloREN establishes a supervised, interpretable framework for sample representation 905 

in single-cell analysis. By uniting biologically informed graph modeling with attention-based 906 

interpretability, FloREN reframes sample representation as a mechanistically grounded discovery 907 

task, enabling scalable identification of the cellular programs and regulatory networks that underlie 908 

complex human diseases. 909

Sample Representations Evaluation Settings 1241 

> 1242

The SPARE metrics (Sample Preservation and Representation Evaluation) 53 are a set of three 1243 

complementary scores designed to quantitatively assess how well a low-dimensional sample 1244 

embedding preserves biologically meaningful structure while removing unwanted technical 1245 

variation. 1246 

> 1247

Information retention & batch effect removal measures how strongly relevant biological/clinical 1248 

covariates (e.g., disease status) are preserved in the embedding and how completely technical 1249 

> .CC-BY-NC-ND 4.0 International license made available under a (which was not certified by peer review) is the author/funder, who has granted bioRxiv a license to display the preprint in perpetuity. It is The copyright holder for this preprint this version posted July 20, 2026. ;https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.07.12.738088 doi: bioRxiv preprint

covariates (e.g., batch) are removed. It uses k=3 nearest-neighbor classification/regression to 1250 

predict each sample’s covariate value from its neighbors in embedding space, then computes a 1251 

corrected macro-F1 score (clipped at 0) for categorical variables or absolute Spearman correlation 1252 

for continuous/ordinal ones; relevant scores are averaged directly, while technical (batch) scores 1253 

are inverted (1 – retention) so higher values mean better removal. 1254 

> 1255

Biological trajectory preservation evaluates whether continuous processes (e.g., age) are correctly 1256 

ordered in the embedding. It computes diffusion pseudotime starting from the sample with the 1257 

minimal value of the trajectory covariate (assumed earliest point), then reports the absolute 1258 

Spearman correlation between this pseudotime and the true covariate value. 1259 

> 1260

Replicate robustness checks whether biologically replicate samples (e.g., multiple samples from the 1261 

same patient/individual) are placed closer to each other in the embedding than to non-replicates. 1262 

For each replicate pair, it calculates the fraction of all other samples that are farther from one 1263 

replicate than its pair partner is, averages over both directions of the pair, and finally averages 1264 

across all such pairs. 1265 

> 1266

These three components are combined into a single aggregated SPARE score using a weighted 1267 

average (information retention and trajectory preservation at weight 1, replicate robustness at 1268 

weight 1, batch removal at weight 0.5 to avoid biasing toward random-like embeddings), normalized 1269 

by the total weight (3.5) to produce a final value in [0,1], where higher is better. 1270 

> 1271

For the results, we used Disease as relevant information retention, we used Batch for irrelevant 1272 

information removal, we used Age for trajectory conservation, and we used Sex replicate 1273 

robustness. 1274

> 1391

FloREN learns an attention score for every edge in the heterogeneous graph of each patient, 1392

patients within each condition to generate condition-specific cell-cell communication profiles, 1401
