Abstract 14 

Chronic stimulation of CD8⁺ T cells within the tumor microenvironment (TME) induces a 15 hypofunctional state characterized by diminished cytotoxicity and functionally impaired anti-16 tumor function, known as exhaustion. Exhaustion is associated with epigenetic changes that 17 remain relatively stable despite interventions like immune checkpoint inhibition (ICI). Although 18 epigenetic changes are potentially reversible, reports of therapeutic strategies to effectively 19 restore function in exhausted CD8 ⁺ T cells remain limited. Here, we report DNA 20 methyltransferase 1 (DNMT1) inhibition (DNMT1i) in counteracting CD8 + T cell dysfunction 21 during the anti-tumor response. We show that DNMT1i synergizes with ICI to rescue the tumor 22 cell killing activity of chronically stimulated CD8⁺ T cells in a melanoma model. DNMT1i 23 mitigates transcriptional features of exhaustion while inducing a divergent effector program. 24 DNMT1i attenuates the global increase in chromatin accessibility associated with exhaustion and 25 enables epigenetic remodeling of the exhausted chromatin landscape upon restimulation. Finally, 26 DNMT1i enhances the effector function of melanoma patient-derived tumor infiltrating 27 lymphocytes after prolonged ex vivo expansion. These studies establish DNMT1 targeting as a 28 promising strategy to counteract CD8⁺ T cell exhaustion and potentiate ICI efficacy. 29 30 

Introduction 31 The advent of ICI has transformed the therapeutic landscape of melanoma. Despite these 32 advances, approximately half of patients with advanced melanoma treated with ICI do not derive 33 long-term benefit ( 1). CD8⁺ T cell function underlies ICI responses, and T cell exhaustion in this 34 compartment limits the responsiveness to ICI ( 2, 3 ). Our group and others have identified a 35 subpopulation of functional memory anti-tumor CD8⁺ T cells that express interleukin-7 receptor 36 (IL7R) that mediate superior tumor control in response to immune checkpoint blockade ( 4, 5 ). 37 IL7R hi anti-tumor CD8⁺ T cells retain high expression of the transcription factor gene Tcf7 , the 38 ability to secrete effector cytokines, and, notably, a poised chromatin landscape with increased 39 accessibility at the promoter of genes associated with cytotoxicity and memory relative to 40 exhausted populations ( 4). These IL7R ⁺ cells overlap functionally with progenitor exhausted 41 (T PEX /TX p) CD8 ⁺ T cells, the self-renewing PD1 ⁺TCF1 ⁺TOX ⁺ population that fuels the 42 proliferative response to checkpoint blockade ( 6) However, progenitor frequency and effector 43 output can be uncoupled, and strategies that expand progenitor-marked cells do not necessarily 44 restore function, underscoring the need for interventions evaluated by functional rescue rather 45 than marker frequency alone. 46

47 Epigenetic plasticity is a key feature of anti-tumor CD8⁺ T cells with functional potential. 48 Chronic antigen stimulation and other tumor microenvironment (TME)-intrinsic cues initiate 49 epigenetic changes in CD8⁺ T cells that precipitate terminal differentiation and preclude anti-50 tumor function in response to ICI ( 7-9 ). Selection and expansion of patient-specific IL7R hi CD8⁺ 51 T cells for adoptive cell therapy (ACT) is an attractive potential therapeutic strategy for 52 melanoma, but IL7R hi anti-tumor CD8⁺ T cells are rare relative to exhausted IL7R lo anti-tumor 53 CD8⁺ T cells ( 4). Given that Tcf7 promoter accessibility is in part regulated by DNA 54 methylation, we have previously found that treating IL7R lo CD8⁺ T cells from tumor-draining 55 lymph nodes with a DNA methyltransferase inhibitor can rescue their anti-tumor function ( 4). 56 Among DNA methyltransferases, DNMT3A has been identified as a key driver of de novo 57 

methylation at stemness-associated loci, including the Tcf7 promoter ( 10 ). DNMT3B, on the 58 other hand, shows reduced expression in mature lymphocytes compared to DNMT3A and 59 DNMT1 ( 11 ). The role of DNMT1, the maintenance methyltransferase responsible for 60 propagating existing methylation marks during cell division, in CD8+ T cell exhaustion is yet 61 unclear. 62 63 Here, we reveal a previously unappreciated role for DNMT1 in restraining anti-tumor function in 64 chronically stimulated CD8⁺ T cells, which can be pharmacologically inhibited. We demonstrate 65 that DNMT1i combined with ICI improves anti-tumor responses in a melanoma model and 66 characterize the transcriptional and epigenetic changes driven by DNMT1 inhibition in 67 chronically stimulated CD8⁺ T cells in vitro . Finally, patient-derived tumor-infiltrating 68 lymphocytes (TILs) undergoing rapid expansion (REP) for melanoma ACT subjected to 69 DNMT1i restore cytokine production and transcriptional features of cytotoxicity. Our findings 70 delineate a novel role for DNMT1 in CD8⁺ T cell exhaustion in the context of chronic 71 stimulation and propose DNMT1i as a strategy to maintain cytotoxic potential of anti-tumor 72 CD8⁺ T cells in melanoma ICI. 73 74

DNMT1 inhibition restores function of chronically stimulated CD8⁺ T cells 162 

Our in vivo findings suggested that DNMT1 inhibition promoted the formation of T PEX from 163 naïve-like precursors during the ICI response, implicating DNMT1 in regulating anti-tumor 164 CD8 ⁺ T cell differentiation. Terminal differentiation and dysfunction of CD8⁺ T cells in the anti-165 tumor response are associated with chronic exposure to antigens and other TME-specific factors 166 (22, 23 ). To further evaluate the role of DNMT1i on CD8⁺ T cell function in the context of 167 dysfunction-promoting chronic stimulation, we adapted a previously reported in vitro model of 168 induction of dysfunction ( 24 ). Naïve, monoclonal gp33-reactive CD8⁺ T cells were isolated from 169 P14 transgenic mice and stimulated with dendritic cells pulsed with gp33 peptide and cultured 170 for one week with or without phorbol 12-myristate 13-acetate (PMA) and with or without 171 DNMT1i ( 24 ) (Fig 2A). PMA activates protein kinase C (PKC) downstream of the T cell 172 receptor, and chronic signaling following activation drives terminal differentiation and 173 exhaustion ( 24 ). After one week of “rest” or chronic stimulation with PMA following initial 174 priming with peptide-pulsed dendritic cells, CD8⁺ T cells were re-challenged with gp33 antigen 175 to assess cytokine release or assessed for capacity to kill YUMM-gp33 cells. In vitro experiments with DNMT1i thus far have involved continuously treating with DNMT1i 206 concurrently with chronic stimulation. We next tested the ability of DNMT1i to attenuate 207 functional exhaustion when provided prior to or after chronic stimulation. Anti-tumor CD8⁺ T 208 cells can acquire dysfunction-associated epigenetic programs as early as 6 hours following 209 activation, and these changes are reinforced progressively over days ( 25 ). DNMT1i regulates TF activity of CD8⁺ T cells under chronic stimulation 351 

Our data showed that DNMT1 inhibition in exhausted T cells induced focal re-patterning in 352 chromatin accessibility of key regulatory loci (concentrated at memory- and exhaustion-353 associated loci) that likely drove global transcriptional remodeling and strong functional rescue. 354 To characterize transcription factor (TF) families involved in regulatory changes induced by 355 DNMT1i, we trained ChromBPNet models, a deep learning architecture for motif footprinting, 356 and determined the frequency of learned motifs in peaks near genes ( 31 ) (Fig 3L) . Discussion 502 

503 

The differentiation of CD8⁺ T cells proceeds along a continuum from naïve to effector, memory, 504 and exhausted states, with epigenetic mechanisms encoding both durable protective immunity 505 and dysfunctional responses under chronic stimulation. In acute infection and CAR-T cell 506 models, a period of antigen withdrawal and cellular quiescence after priming is essential for the 507 acquisition of memory whereas persistent stimulation diverts cells into an exhausted fate 508 characterized by diminished recall and restricted proliferative potential ( 9, 30, 34-37 ). Exhausted 509 CD8⁺ T cells display extensive chromatin remodeling relative to memory cells, and prior work 510 has shown that neither antigen withdrawal nor immune checkpoint blockade fully restores a 511 memory-like epigenetic landscape once exhaustion is established ( 30, 38, 39 ). These 512 observations have led to the view that exhausted CD8⁺ T cells are epigenetically “scarred” and 513 largely fixed in their differentiation trajectory( 30 ). 514 515 We investigate whether interfering with maintenance DNA methylation can alter the trajectory of 516 CD8⁺ T cell differentiation and enhance anti-tumor immunity. We show in a melanoma model 517 that pharmacologic hypomethylation via DNMT1i synergizes with CTLA4-directed ICI to enrich 518 a progenitor-exhausted (T PEX ) CD8⁺ population in the tdLN that is transcriptionally and 519 functionally similar to human checkpoint-responsive T PEX . In vivo , combining DNMT1i with 520 immune checkpoint blockade shifts the tdLN CD8⁺ compartment away from naïve-like 521 precursors towards T PEX without significantly altering the frequency of T EFF EX in the tumor or 522 tdLN. The latter finding may reflect differences in the lifespan or trafficking equilibrium of 523 terminal effectors. These data support a model that DNMT1i biases early fate choices, favoring 524 the generation and maintenance of a resource T PEX pool that can fuel effective effector responses 525 under checkpoint blockade. 526 527 Our in vitro model further demonstrates that DNMT1i rescues the function of chronically 528 stimulated CD8⁺ T cells by inducing a divergent transcriptional and regulatory state rather than 529 returning cells to a canonical memory-like fate. Chronic PKC-dependent signaling drives a 530 typical exhausted phenotype with diminished TNFα/IFNγ production and cytotoxicity despite 531 increased proliferation and robust expression of exhaustion- and effector-associated genes. 532 DNMT1i during this chronic stimulation preserves dual TNFα⁺IFNγ⁺ producers and restores 533 tumor killing, even when drug exposure was either limited to the first 36 hours after priming or 534 after chronic stimulation. These findings indicate that DNMT1 can act both early during repeated 535 stimulation and after chronic stimulation signals have already been integrated to imprint 536 exhaustion-associated programs and that transient interference with DNMT1 activity is sufficient 537 to protect effector potential. 538 539 Transcriptionally, DNMT1i redirects exhausted CD8⁺ T cells away from activation- and terminal 540 effector-associated programs towards stemness- and early effector-like modules. Bulk RNA Sequencing of P14 CD8 T Cells 784 CD8 ⁺ T cells were harvested from in vitro cultures under the indicated stimulation and drug 785 conditions in triplicates. Total RNA was extracted using the RNeasy kit (Qiagen). RNA quality 786 and concentration were assessed prior to library preparation. Sequencing 788 was performed with paired-end reads to sufficient depth for differential expression analysis. 789 Transcript-level abundances were quantified using kallisto and differential expression analysis 790 was performed using DESeq2. Genes with low counts were filtered prior to analysis (min. 5 791 reads in at least 2 samples). DE testing was carried out using the Wald test. LFC shrinkage was 792 performed using the ashr method. Genes were considered differentially expressed based on 793 adjusted p values (Benjamini–Hochberg correction) and effect size thresholds as specified in 794 figure legends. Gene set enrichment analysis was performed on differential expression results 795 using the clusterProfiler package with gene sets derived from MSigDB, including Hallmark (H), 796 curated (C2), and immunologic (C7) collections. To identify latent transcriptional programs 797 across conditions, consensus non-negative matrix factorization (cNMF) was applied to 798 normalized gene expression data. Normalized DESeq2 counts were used as input after selecting 799 the top 2,000 most variable genes across samples. Ribosomal, mitochondrial, and housekeeping 800 genes were excluded prior to factorization. The optimal number of modules was determined by 801 rank estimation across ranks 2–8 using repeated runs of the Brunet algorithm. A four-module 802 solution was selected based on stability and interpretability, and NMF was run with 50 iterations 803 to obtain robust gene-by-module and sample-by-module matrices. To compare DNMT1i treatment of P14 806 CD8⁺ T cells to DNMT1KO in human T cells, differentially expressed genes (adjusted p < 0.05) 807 from the drug-treated dataset and the published knockout dataset from Li et al. were compared. 808 To formally assess whether differential expression in the drug-treated dataset was enriched 809 among genes differentially expressed in the knockout dataset, analysis was restricted to the set of 810 genes shared between both datasets. Each gene was classified as differentially expressed or not 811 (padj < 0.05). A contingency table was constructed comparing differential expression status 812 across datasets, and statistical significance of the overlap was assessed using a chi-squared test of 813 independence. 814 815
