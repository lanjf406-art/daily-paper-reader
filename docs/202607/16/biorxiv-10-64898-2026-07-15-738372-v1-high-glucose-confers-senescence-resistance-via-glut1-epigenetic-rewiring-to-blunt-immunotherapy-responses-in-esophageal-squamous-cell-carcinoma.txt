Abstract 37 

Therapeutic resistance and undefined predictive biomarkers severely hinder the 38 

clinical popularization of immunotherapy in esophageal squamous cell carcinoma 39 

(ESCC). Herein, we identify the glucose transporter 1 (GLUT1) as a critical 40 

determinant of immunotherapy resistance. Elevated expression of GLUT1 correlates 41 

with poor immunotherapy response and unfavorable prognosis in ESCC patients. 42 

GLUT1 deletion or inhibition enhances CD8⁺ T cell infiltration and cytotoxicity, and 43 

sensitizes ESCC tumors to anti-PD-1 (α-PD1) therapy. Importantly, dietary glucose 44 

restriction exhibits equivalent antitumor efficacy to GLUT1 inhibition when combined 45 

with α-PD1. Mechanistically, GLUT1 establishes a positive feedback loop with HAT1 46 

and FOXM1, which epigenetically remodels chromatin accessibility to suppress tumor 47 

cell senescence, thereby impeding CD8⁺ T cell-mediated antitumor immunity. Our 48 

findings highlight GLUT1 as a predictive biomarker of immunotherapy resistance and 49 

suggest dietary glucose restriction as a viable strategy to potentiate immunotherapy 50 

efficacy in ESCC. 51

Main 53 

Esophageal squamous cell carcinoma (ESCC) remains a major cause of cancer 54 

mortality in East and Central Asia, especially in China 1. Despite advances in 55 

multimodality management, including surgery, radiotherapy, and chemotherapy, 56 

clinical outcomes remain poor due to lack of specific therapeutic targets and the 57 

heterogeneity of the ESCC tumor microenvironment (TME) 1,2 . In recent years, 58 

immunotherapy has brought substantial advancements in the treatment of various solid 59 

tumors 3-5 . Notably, PD-1/PD-L1 blockade combined with chemotherapy has become 60 

the standard first-line and even neoadjuvant regimen for advanced ESCC 6-8 . However, 61 

clinical responses remain limited to a subset of patients 9. Therefore, it is imperative to 62 

elucidate the molecular mechanisms underlying immune responsiveness and to develop 63 

predictive biomarkers for identifying eligible beneficiaries and optimizing therapeutic 64 

strategies. 65

connecting tumor cell senescence to tumor immunity remain largely underappreciated. 77 

GLUT1 (glucose transporter 1), encoded by SLC2A1 , is a high-affinity glucose 78 

transporter 21 . As a core regulator of tumor metabolic reprogramming, GLUT1 plays an 79 

indispensable role in the development and immune evasion of tumors, as well as 80 

therapy resistance. Studies in glioblastoma have demonstrated that GLUT1 not only 81 

promotes glycolysis and malignant progression through its S-palmitoylation 22 , but also 82 

facilitates immune evasion by enhancing immunosuppressive activity of monocyte-83 

derived macrophages via glucose-driven histone lactylation, and upregulating PD-L1 84 

expression through HK2-mediated NF-κB activation 23,24 . In melanoma, enhanced 85 

glycolysis in tumor cells is one of the key characteristics leading to immune resistance 86 

to adoptive T cell therapy 25 . Although several studies have reported the high expression 87 

of GLUT1 in ESCC 26,27 , the mechanistic role of GLUT1, particularly in regulating 88 

tumor cell senescence-mediated immune responses, has not been fully characterized. 89 

In this study, we demonstrate that GLUT1, a key regulator of glucose metabolism, 90 

promotes tumor immune evasion by resisting tumor cell senescence in ESCC. Clinically, 91 

high GLUT1 expression independently predicts poor prognosis and confers 92 

immunochemotherapy resistance in ESCC patients. Mechanistically, GLUT1 forms a 93 

positive feedback loop with HAT1 and FOXM1, thereby cooperatively driving 94 

epigenetic remodeling of chromatin accessibility to suppress tumor cell senescence and 95 

senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP) chemokine production, 96

Results 104 

Elevated expression of GLUT1 in tumor tissues correlates with poor response to 105 

immunotherapy in ESCC patients 106 

To systematically identify potential predictive and functional biomarkers 107 

associated with immunotherapy resistance in ESCC, we first analyzed publicly 108 

available scRNA-seq data of 42 pretreatment patient samples from three independent 109 

ESCC cohorts (Discovery cohort: HRA003312, HRA004396, and HRA004740) 28-30 ,110 

in which patients received neoadjuvant anti-PD-1 (α-PD1) therapy plus chemotherapy 111 

(Figure 1A). We identified 319 genes that could effectively distinguish non-complete 112 

response (NCR) from pathological complete response (CR). Of these, 116 candidate 113 

genes exhibited elevated expression in tumor epithelium relative to normal counterparts. 114 

Notably, correlation analysis revealed that GLUT1 showed the strongest negative 115 

correlation with CD8⁺ T cell infiltration (Figures 1B and S1A). 116 

To further investigate the clinical relevance of GLUT1 in ESCC, we integrated 117 

data from 42 scRNA-seq datasets, protein expression data from 137 in-house FFPE 118 

tissue specimens, and 1,119 bulk RNA-seq samples. First, GLUT1 had remarkably high 119 

expression in epithelial cells, especially in malignant tumor cells when compared with 120 

other cell types such as immune cells, fibroblasts, or endothelial cells (Figure 1C). 121 

Second, among the 14 GLUT-family members, GLUT1 displayed the highest 122 

expression level and the most significant differential expression between tumor and 123 

paired normal tissues in patients with ESCC, as well as in other types of squamous cell 124 

carcinoma (Figures 1D, 1E, and S1B). Third, GLUT1 expression was gradually 125 

elevated from early to advanced ESCC and positively correlated with clinical stage 126

Consistent with these findings, tumors with low GLUT1 expression and those from 136

accompanied by a reduced infiltration of CD8⁺ T cells (Figure 1J and 1K). This inverse 143 

association was further validated by immunofluorescence (IF) staining using our in-144 

house tissue microarrays derived from patients with ESCC and LUSC (Figures 1L, S1E, 145

significant negative correlation between GLUT1 level and CD8⁺ T cell infiltration 147 

across squamous cell carcinomas (Figure 1M). Thus, these findings identify GLUT1 as 148 

a reliable predictive biomarker for immunotherapy resistance in ESCC, potentially by 149 

suppressing CD8⁺ T cell infiltration. 150

response, we first investigated its role in the tumor microenvironment (TME) by 154 

establishing syngeneic murine ESCC models in immunocompetent C57BL/6J mice. 155 

Notably, Glut1 knockdown significantly suppressed tumor growth in vivo (Figures 2A, 156

GLUT1 suppresses cellular senescence to sustain tumor progression in ESCC 178 

To determine the tumor-intrinsic mechanisms by which GLUT1 impacts CD8⁺ T 179 

cell function, we first investigated the basis for the aberrant upregulation of GLUT1 in 180 

ESCC. Neither frequent mutation nor copy number amplification accounted for 181 

elevated GLUT1 expression in ESCC patients or across 1,119 squamous cell carcinoma 182 

samples (Figure S3A). Instead, we observed a pronounced enrichment of the active 183 

enhancer mark H3K27ac around the GLUT1 locus in both ESCC tissues and cell lines 184 

relative to their normal counterparts, accompanied by high-density occupancy of 185 

RNAPII, H3K4me3, and H3K4me1 (Figure S3B). GLUT1 was then identified as a 186

including KLF5 and SOX2 31,32 (Figure S3C). H3K27ac signal intensity strongly 188 

correlated with GLUT1 mRNA and protein expression across examined ESCC cell 189 

lines (Figure S3D), indicating that SE-driven transcriptional activation leads to aberrant 190 

GLUT1 expression in ESCC. 191 

We then performed a series of functional experiments to interrogate the functional 192 

consequences of GLUT1 in both human and murine ESCC tumor cells. High GLUT1 193 

expression was detected in 8 of the 12 ESCC cell lines examined, including KYSE140, 194 

T.T, KYSE70, and TE-1 (Figure S4A). Transcriptome sequencing analysis using wild-195 

type (WT) and GLUT1 -KO TE-1 cells showed that 294 genes were commonly 196 

downregulated after GLUT1 knockout (two independent gRNA targets exhibited high 197 

consistency) when compared with the WT control (Figure 3A). Further KEGG 198 

enrichment analysis of the downregulated genes revealed cellular senescence as the 199 

most significantly affected pathway by GLUT1 knockout, followed by aging-related 200 

diseases, such as Parkinson’s, Alzheimer’s, and Huntington’s diseases. Moreover, most 201 

genes in these pathways have been reported to function as anti-senescence regulators, 202 

including AKT1, FOXM1, and HIPK1 (Figure 3B), suggesting that GLUT1 may 203 

contribute to the resistance of ESCC cells to cellular senescence. 204 

Indeed, GLUT1 deletion or pharmacological inhibition with the GLUT1-selective 205 

compound BAY-876 induced a pronounced senescent phenotype, characterized by 206 

enlarged, flattened morphology, increased SA-β-Gal activity, and higher expression of 207 

senescence markers (p53, p27, p16) (Figure 3C and 3D). Moreover, deletion of GLUT1 208 

markedly inhibited cell proliferation, invasion, and colony formation in GLUT1 -high 209 

human TE-1 and KYSE140, as well as murine HNM007 cells (Figure S4B-S4E). 210 

Conversely, overexpression of GLUT1 enhanced these malignant phenotypes in 211 

GLUT1 -low KYSE30 and KYSE450 cells (Figure S4F-S4G). Importantly, these effects 212 

were faithfully restored by exogenous overexpression of GLUT1 in GLUT1 -knockout 213 

(KO) cells (Figure S5A-S5I). Furthermore, pharmacological inhibition with BAY-876 214 

suppressed malignant phenotypes and induced senescence (Figure S5J-S5P), indicating 215 

that senescence induction by targeting GLUT1 reduces cellular oncogenicity of ESCC. 216

Consistent with the in vitro findings, GLUT1 -KO tumors exhibited significantly 221 

reduced tumor volume, decreased Ki67 staining, and upregulated expression of 222 

senescence-associated proteins compared with the WT counterparts in both murine 223 

ESCC xenograft and allograft models (Figures 3E, 3F, and S5X). Given the clinical 224 

relevance of GLUT1 expression described above, we generated patient-derived 225 

organoids (PDOs) from ESCC patients and validated the induction of cellular 226 

senescence with the GLUT1 inhibitor, BAY-876. Compared with corresponding normal 227 

organoids, elevated expression of GLUT1 at both mRNA and protein levels was 228 

observed in 4 out of 6 PDOs (Figure 3G and 3H). Notably, tumor organoids with higher 229 

GLUT1 expression (from patient 4) exhibited greater sensitivity to BAY-876 and a more 230 

pronounced senescent phenotype relative to its normal organoids. However, such a 231 

difference was not observed in organoids derived from patient 6, which had low 232 

expression of GLUT1 (Figure 3H-3J). Consistent results were obtained in mouse tissue-233 

derived organoids. Low-dose BAY-876 triggered a remarkable senescent phenotype and 234 

significantly inhibited the growth of ESCC tumor organoids, while exerting no obvious 235 

effect on normal esophageal organoids (Figures 3K, 3L, and S5Y). These results 236 

demonstrate that GLUT1 suppresses cellular senescence in ESCC to facilitate tumor 237 

progression, and suggest that high expression of GLUT1 may serve as a potential 238 

predictor of sensitivity to BAY-876 treatment. 239

GLUT1 forms a positive feedback loop with HAT1 and FOXM1 cooperatively 241 

promoting chromatin accessibility to antagonize tumor cell senescence 242 

To elucidate the molecular mechanism by which GLUT1 regulates tumor cell 243 

senescence, we verified the expression of anti‑senescence genes identified from our 244 

transcriptome data in GLUT1 ‑KO TE-1 and KYSE140 cells (Figure 3B). As expected, 245 

GLUT1 deletion resulted in significant downregulation of these established anti-246

S6B). In addition, we found that HAT1, a histone acetyltransferase implicated in 248 

chromatin remodeling and transcription factor recruitment, was also downregulated 249

potentially via modulating GLUT1-HAT1/FOXM1 axis 378

Discussion 474 

Both GLUT1-mediated glycolysis 23,44-46 and cellular senescence 19,20 have been 475 

independently confirmed to regulate immune responses in both tumor cells and immune 476 

cells. In this study, we demonstrate GLUT1 as a key mediator in ESCC tumor cells 477 

connecting glucose metabolism to cellular senescence and antitumor immunity, 478 

representing a novel function of GLUT1 beyond glucose transport. Herein, GLUT1 in 479 

ESCC tumor cells can remodel TME and suppress adaptive antitumor immune response 480 

by resisting cellular senescence, in which nuclear HAT1/FOXM1 anti-senescence axis 481 

is a key downstream node of GLUT1 signaling. In GLUT1-high ESCC tumors, anti-482 

senescent genes, including FOXM1, were transcriptionally activated upon glucose 483 

uptake, while SASP chemokines responsible for CD8⁺ T cell recruitment, such as CCL5, 484 

CXCL10, and CXCL11 were inhibited through remodeling of chromatin accessibility. 485 

Deletion of GLUT1 or treatment with BAY-876, α-PD1 alone or their combination 486

senescence pathway in T cell-mediated immune responses, the concurrent alterations 490 

observed in macrophages and NK cells suggest the involvement of additional, yet 491 

uncharacterized mechanisms. Future studies are warranted to delineate how GLUT1-492 

mediated anti-senescence signaling interacts with distinct immune cell populations, 493 

either directly or indirectly, to establish an immune-evasive TME. 494 

Our findings carry significant implications for the clinical management of ESCC. 495 

We identify GLUT1 as a reliable biomarker to predict non-responsiveness to 496 

immunotherapy and unfavorable prognosis in patients with ESCC. Consistently, the 497 

significant correlation of high GLUT1 expression with advanced stage, increased 498

their normal counterparts. Moreover, GLUT1 is significantly upregulated in 502 

immunotherapy nonresponsive ESCC patients, possessing a superior discriminatory 503 

performance relative to PD-L1. 504 

Therapeutically, strategies targeting the GLUT1-regulated immune response hold 505

Limitations of the study 557 

We revealed a negative correlation between GLUT1 expression and 558 

immunotherapy response based on scRNA-seq data from 42 ESCC patients, and further 559 

validated this association using tissue specimens from an independent cohort of 25 560 

ESCC patients receiving immunochemotherapy. Nevertheless, the generalizability of 561 

GLUT1 as a stratification biomarker for immunotherapy non-response remains to be 562 

validated in large clinical cohorts of ESCC and other squamous cell carcinomas. While 563 

results from our murine ESCC model and patient-derived ESCC organoid co-culture 564 

systems support that dietary glucose restriction potentiates immunotherapy efficacy, 565 

optimized immunotherapy strategies based on dietary intervention still require further 566 

evaluation in clinical trials. Additionally, given the ubiquitous involvement of GLUT1 567 

and glucose metabolism in tumor progression, our findings merit further translational 568 

exploration across pan-cancer settings. 569
