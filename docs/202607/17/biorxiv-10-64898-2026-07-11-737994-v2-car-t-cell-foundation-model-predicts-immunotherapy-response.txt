Abstract 16 

Single -cell transcriptomics resolves CAR T -cell states, yet translating heterogeneous cellular 17 

signals into patient -level therapeutic response remains challenging . Existing studies primarily 18 

identify response -associated genes or cell populations through experimental and statistical 19 

analyses , but few predictive frameworks integrate gene -level structure with clinical outcomes. 20 

Here, we present gANCHOR, a T-cell foundation model built on a hierarchical hypergraph 21 

attention framework combining biologically informed representation learning with patient -22 

level response prediction. By encoding gene -pathway relationships, gANCHOR learns 23 

pathway -aware cell embeddings that improve biological conservation and batch robustness. A 24 

cell -to -patient attention module then aggregates cellular information to infer therapeutic 25 

response. Across benchmark datasets , gANCHOR achieve d the strongest overall performance 26 

in biological conservation and batch -correction assessments . In response prediction across 161 27 

patients from five CAR T -cell studies , gANCHOR achieved an F1 score of 0.87 , outperforming 28 

benchmarked single -cell foundation models . gANCHOR also identifie d reproducible response -29 

and non -response -associated gene programs , providing interpretable biological insights into 30 

CAR T -cell efficacy and resistance .31

Introduction 33 

Chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T -cell therapy, in which patient -derived T lymphocytes are 34 

genetically engineered to target tumor -associated antigens, has transformed cancer treatment 35 

by producing unprecedented responses in otherwise refractory hematological malignancies 1.36 

By combining an antibody -derived antigen recognition domain with intracellular T -cell 37

Advances in single -cell RNA sequencing (scRNA -seq) have provided high -resolution 45

models of tumor -immune interactions .55 

Despite these advances, predictive modeling of CAR T -cell therapeutic response remains 56

capture only a fraction of the variability in patient outcomes 14 -16 . Although r ecent scRNA -seq 58

persistence, and dysfunction 17 -30 , these analyses are predominantly descriptive and correlation -60

Results 81 

gANCHOR establishes a hierarchical framework for CAR T response prediction 82 

We developed gANCH OR (graph Attention Network for CAR -T Hierarchical Outcome 83 

Reasoning ), a deep learning framework designed to infer patient -level therapeutic outcomes 84 

from CAR T single -cell transcriptomes (Figure 1 ). gANCH OR adopts a hierarchical two -85 

module architecture in which Module I learns biologically informed cell embeddings and 86 

Module II performs attention -based patient -level response prediction. This framework enables 87 

modeling of pathway -guided gene relationships within cells and cellular heterogeneity across 88 

patients while preserving interpretability through hierarchical attention mechanisms .89 

Module I serves as a single -cell foundation model trained on integrated CAR T -cell and 90 

reference T -cell datasets . Rather than treating genes as independent features, it incorporates 91 

curated gene -pathway relationships through a hypergraph attention framework . This 92 

architecture involves a gene encoder, a cell encoder, and a cell decoder. In the gene encoder, a 93 

bipartite gene -pathway graph is constructed using Molecular Signatures Database (MSigDB) 94 

> 31

, and hypergraph attention layers generate pathway -aware gene embeddings as structured 95 

biological priors . The cell encoder then integrates single -cell gene expression with gene 96 

embeddings to capture pathway -informed transcriptional dependencies while preserving cell -97 

to -cell heterogeneity. The cell decoder further optimizes these representations through multi -98 

task objectives, including gene expression reconstruction and cell -type prediction, while an 99 

adversarial batch discriminator reduces dataset -specific technical variation. This training 100 

strategy yields transferable and batch -corrected cell embeddings. 101 

Module II uses the learned cell embeddings to predict patient -level response. A cell -to -102 

patient attention mechanism dynamically weights individual cells and aggregates them into a 103 

unified patient representation for downstream classification. Training is performed in two 104 

independent stages. Module I is first pretrained to learn transferable cell embeddings, after 105 

which the encoder is fixed and Module II is then trained separately for patient -level prediction .106 

This strategy stabilizes optimization and reduces overfitting to limited clinical outcome labels. 107

gANCHOR improves patient -level CAR T response prediction 135 

Given the strong biological conservation and batch -correction performance of gANCHOR 136 

embeddings, we next evaluated whether these representations improve patient -level CAR T 137 

therapy response prediction (Figure 3A, B ). For all single cell foundation models, including 138 

gANCHOR, pretrained cell embeddings were used as fixed inputs to a unified prediction 139 

framework (Module II ), ensuring that performance differences primarily reflect representation 140 

quality rather than classifier design. Raw gene expression profiles were also included as an 141 

additional baseline , allowing direct comparison between learned representations and 142 

unprocessed transcriptomic features. 143 

Across all models and input settings, gANCHOR consistently achieved the best predictive 144 

performance on the held -out test data . It attained the highest F1 score (Figure 3B ; Figure S 3)145 

while maintaining robust performance across complementary evaluation metrics, including 146 

precision, recall, AUROC, and AUPRC ( Figure S 4), demonstrating balanced discrimination 147 

between responder and non -responder patients . Notably, raw gene expression profiles 148 

performed substantially worse than embedding -based approaches, highlighting the importance 149 

of representation learning for capturing predictive cellular features. To further assess the 150 

contribution of cell -level modeling, we performed an additional ablation by collapsing single -151 

cell data into patient -level features via mean aggregation of gene expression across all cells, 152 

followed by logistic regression for response prediction ( Figure 3B). This simplified baseline 153

gANCHOR identifies reproducible gene programs associated with clinical response 166 

Having established that gANCHOR accurately predicts patient -level therapeutic response, we 167 

next investigated the molecular programs underlying these predictions by quantifying gene -168 

level contributions through the hierarchical attention structure of the model. Patient -specific 169 

gene attention scores were computed by propagating importance from genes to cells and 170 

subsequently to patients , enabling identification of differential attention -enriched genes 171 

(DA EGs) between responding and non -responding patients (see Methods). Across four 172 

independent training repeats (Table S3 ), we identified top 100 DA EGs for each group, with 59 173 

genes in the responding group (Figure 3C, E ) and 65 genes in the non -responding group 174 

(Figure 3D, F ) consistently identified across all runs . These gene sets reflect distinct immune 175 

states and provide a robust basis for interpreting the molecular determinants of CAR T -cell 176 

therapeutic outcomes. 177 

In the responding group, DA EGs were enriched for genes associated with T cell activation, 178 

memory formation, and cytotoxic effector function (Figure 3E). For example, SELL (CD62L), 179 

IL7R (CD127), and CD27 are canonical markers of central memory and stem -like T cells that 180 

have been consistently linked to improved CAR T expansion and persistence. XCL1 is involved 181 

in chemokine -mediated immune cell recruitment and coordination, whereas KLRK1 (NKG2D) 182 

and FGFBP2 mark highly cytotoxic effector programs . PTPRC underscores the importance of 183 

intact T -cell receptor signaling in therapeutic efficacy. Together , these genes indicate a 184 

functionally competent, memory -enriched, and cytotoxic CAR T -cell state associated with 185 

favorable outcomes. 186 

In contrast, DA EGs enriched in non -responding patients exhibited signatures of T cell 187 

dysfunction and exhaustion (Figure 3F). Immune checkpoint molecules such as PDCD1 (PD -188 

1), CTLA4 , and TIGIT are well -established exhausted T cell markers, and have been linked to 189 

impaired CAR T expansion and reduced antitumor activity. Transcription factors JUN , JUNB ,190 

and FOS , components of the AP -1 complex, are associated with chronic activation and stress 191 

responses. Enrichment of FOXP3 suggests increased regulatory T cell -like programs 192

gANCHOR learns biologically structured and interpretable gene and cell representations 206 

Beyond patient -level response prediction, we further examined whether gANCHOR captures 207 

biologically meaningful cellular and gene -level organization within the learned representation 208 

space. To assess cell -level representations, we examined the model’s ability to classify T cell 209 

subtypes. The model achieved robust classification performance, with the confusion matrix 210 

showing accurate discrimination of major T cell populations while preserving expected 211 

similarities among closely related states ( Figure 4A, B; Figure S 5). These results indicate that 212 

the learned cell embeddings encode biologically relevant transcriptional programs and support 213 

reliable cell identity resolution. 214 

To assess gene -level organization, we analyzed relationships among genes in the learned 215 

embedding space by comparing pairwise distances between genes belonging to the same 216 

MSigDB pathways with distances from randomly sampled gene sets . Genes within the same 217 

pathway exhibited significantly smaller pairwise distances ( Wilcoxon rank -sum test , P < 0.001) 218 

(Figure 4C, D; Figure S 6), indicating that the learned embeddings preserve pathway -level 219 

functional coherence . This structured organization suggests that genes participating in shared 220 

biological processes are embedded in close proximity within the latent space .221 

To further evaluate interpretability, we analyzed gene -to -cell attention weights and 222 

identified DA EGs for each T cell subtype (see Methods). These subtype -specific DA EGs 223 

revealed strong concordance with established marker genes, with most canonical markers 224 

exhibiting positive log 2 fold change and high statistical significance, indicating consistently 225 

elevated attention within their corresponding cell types ( Figure 4E, F). Cytotoxic T cell subsets ,226 

including CD8 -positive and CD4 -positive cytotoxic populations , show ed increased attention to 227 

effector molecules such as GZMB , PRF1 , GNLY , and NKG7 , consistent with cytolytic function .228 

Memory T cell subsets display ed enriched attention to IL7R and CCR7 , reflecting lymphoid 229 

homing and long -term persistence. Regulatory T cells exhibited strong and specific attention to 230 

FOXP3 , IL2RA , and CTLA4 , capturing their immunosuppressive phenotype. Activated T cell 231

Attention -derived representations preserve biologically structured cellular states 249 

To assess whether the learned attention mechanisms provide informative representations 250 

beyond embeddings, we evaluated gene -to -cell attention outputs as an alternative feature space 251 

for characterizing cellular states. For each cell, attention -weighted gene representations were 252 

extracted from the gene -to -cell attention module and used for downstream analys es. These 253 

representations revealed coherent organization of annotated T -cell subtypes while improving 254 

dataset mixing (Figure S8A-C). In the integrated reference T-cell and CAR T-cell dataset ,255 

attention -based representations preserved T-cell structure while retaining CAR T-cell 256 

heterogeneity , suggesting that the model avoids overcorrection under domain shift. 257 

Quantitative evaluation further showed that attention -derived representations achieved 258 

biological conservation comparable to normalized gene expression while substantially 259 

improving batch correction (Figure S8D). Although learned cell embeddings remained the 260 

best -performing representation overall, attention -derived features consistently outperformed 261 

raw gene expression in overall integration quality, suggesting that the gene -to -cell attention 262 

mechanism preserves biologically meaningful cellular structure while reducing technical 263 

variation across datasets and retaining gene -level resolution .264 

We next investigated whether attention -derived representations provide biological 265 

information beyond conventional differential expression analysis. Using identical statistical 266 

procedures, we compared DA EGs with differential expression genes (DEGs) identified from 267 

gene expression. At the cell -type level, where batch effects are relatively modest (Figure 2A ),268 

both approaches recovered canonical subtype marker genes with comparable accuracy ( Figure 269 

3E,F and Figure S9), indicating that attention faithfully preserves established transcriptional 270

Discussion 283 

gANCHOR establishes a hierarchical hypergraph attention framework that connects single -cell 284 

transcriptomic representation learning with patient -level therapeutic outcome prediction in 285 

CAR T -cell therapy. By encoding curated gene -pathway relationships as a bipartite hypergraph, 286 

the foundation module learns pathway -aware gene and cell embeddings that preserve 287 

coordinated functional programs while reducing batch effects . The cell -to -patient attention 288 

module then aggregates heterogeneous cellular states into patient -level embeddings for accurate 289 

and interpretable response prediction . Under a unified downstream architecture, gANCHOR 290 

consistently achieves improved predictive performance. The hierarchical attention structure 291 

propagates feature importance from genes to cells and patients, allowing identification of gene 292 

programs associated with therapeutic response. The resulting gene signatures show 293 

concordance with reported CAR T -cell functional states, including activation, memory, 294 

cytotoxicity, and exhaustion, indicating that gANCHOR captures biologically meaningful 295 

signals relevant to therapeutic outcomes. 296 

Beyond response prediction , gANCHOR provides a generalizable framework for linking 297 

single -cell molecular representations to clinically relevant phenotypes across heterogeneous T -298 

cell and CAR T -cell scRNA -seq datasets . By integrating pathway -guided representation 299 

learning with hierarchical attention, the framework enables interpretable mapping from 300 

molecular features to cellular states and patient -level predictions while maintaining robustness 301 

across diverse studies and sequencing platforms . This design supports both response prediction 302 

and identification of clinically relevant genes and pathways . As a T cell -focused foundation 303 

model, gANCHOR learn s reusable representations from large -scale integrated T -cell and CAR 304 

T-cell transcriptomic datasets. These transferable representations and attention -derived scores 305 

provide useful features for biomarker discovery, patient stratification, and task -specific fine -306 

tuning in related immunological settings where T -cell heterogeneity is central. More broadly, 307 

this study illustrates the feasibility of integrating structured biological priors with deep learning 308

gANCHOR also has several limitations . The gene -pathway hypergraph relies on existing 310 

pathway annotations, which may be incomplete or biased toward well -characterized biological 311

attention mechanisms improve interpretability, attention weights do not constitute direct 313

Methods 325 

T cell and CAR T cell single cell data collection and initial filtering 326 

scRNA -seq datasets of T cells and CAR T cells were collected from twelve published studies 327 

spanning multiple disease contexts. All CAR T -cell samples used in this study were derived 328 

from pre -infusion material collected prior to clinical outcome assessment, ensuring that both 329 

representation pretraining (Module I) and patient -level response prediction (Module II) were 330 

performed exclusively using available transcriptomic information. These CAR T -cell datasets 331 

enabled characterization of transcriptional programs associated with subsequent therapeutic 332 

response 17 -19,29,30 . Reference T-cell datasets were obtained from large -scale atlases and disease -333 

focused cohorts and were subsetted to retain annotated T-cell populations across diverse 334 

functional subtypes 42 -48 through the CZ CELLxGENE database 49 . All datasets were processed 335 

using a unified preprocessing workflow , including normalization to account for sequencing 336 

depth and harmonization to a shared gene space where necessary. For datasets lacking raw 337 

counts, expression values were transformed to ensure compatibility with downstream analysis. 338 

For further details on how each dataset was collected, curated, and processed, please see the 339 

Supplementary Information. 340

Gene selection and pathway filtering 365 

gANCHOR pretraining was based on genes with curated pathway annotations. We utilized the 366 

Molecular Signatures Database (MSigDB) 31 curated human gene set collection, specifically 367 

the C2 category, which comprises both Canonical Pathways (CP) and Chemical and Genetic 368 

Perturbations (CGP). These curated gene sets provide structured prior knowledge of gene -369 

pathway relationships derived from experimentally validated biological processes and 370 

perturbation studies . The MSigDB C2 collection contains 21,587 unique genes. Genes lacking 371 

functional pathway annotations were excluded from downstream analysis. To ensure 372 

consistency across datasets, only genes that were detected across all pretraining T cell and CAR 373 

T-cell datasets were retained. Ribosomal genes were further removed to reduce potential 374 

confounding from technical variability and highly abundant housekeeping signals. After 375 

filtering, the final feature space consisted of 11,140 genes, which were used as for hypergraph 376 

construction and downstream representation learning. 377

Single -cell d ata normalization 389 

scRNA -seq data preprocessing was performed using Scanpy . No additional cell - or gene -level 390 

filtering beyond the original study -specific quality -control procedures was applied beyond the 391 

criteria used in the original studies from which the datasets were obtained. Raw count matrices 392 

were first normalized to 10,000 total UMI counts per cell using scanpy.pp.normalize_total, 393 

followed by logarithmic transformation with scanpy.pp.log1p . To further mitigate gene -specific 394 

expression bias and technical variability associated with capture efficiency and sequencing 395 

depth, an additional gene -wise normalization step was applied . For each detected gene, the non -396 

zero median expression value was calculated across all cells in the training datasets containing 397 

both T cells and CAR T cells . Gene expression values in each cell were then divided by the 398 

corresponding non -zero median. This procedure places genes with systematically different 399 

baseline expression levels onto a comparable scale while preserving biologically meaningful 400

Batch effect correction via gradient reversal 765 

To reduce dataset -specific technical variation while preserving biological signal, we adopt ed 766 

an adversarial domain adaptation strategy implemented through a gradient reversal layer 52 . The 767 

batch discriminator was trained to correctly classify batch labels from cell embeddings, whereas 768 

the encoder was simultaneously optimized to prevent accurate batch discrimination . During 769 

backpropagation, gradients from the batch classification loss were multiplied by a negative 770 

constant  −𝛾  before propagating into the encoder. This adversarial optimization encourages 771 

batch -invariant representations while retaining biologically meaningful structure relevant to 772 

downstream prediction tasks .773 

Formally, discriminator parameters were optimized to minimize  𝐿 𝑏𝑎𝑡𝑐 ℎ , while encoder 774 

parameters are optimized to minimize  𝐿 𝑐𝑙𝑠 + 𝐿 𝑟𝑒𝑐  while maximize  𝐿 𝑏𝑎𝑡𝑐 ℎ . The gradient 775 

reversal layer provides a stable implementation of this min -max objective. 776

Model parameters were initialized using Xavier initialization and optimized using the Adam 779 

optimizer. The initial learning rate was set to 0.00001 , with weight decay 0.0001 . Training was 780

To adaptively adjust optimization dynamics during training, we used 788
