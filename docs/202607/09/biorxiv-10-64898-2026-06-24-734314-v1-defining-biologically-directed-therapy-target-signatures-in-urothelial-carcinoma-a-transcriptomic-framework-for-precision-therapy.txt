Statement of Translational Relevance :108 

Enfortumab vedotin plus pembrolizumab has redefined first -line therapy for advanced urothelial 109 

carcinoma, yet treatment selection following resistance or progression remains undefined. In this 110 

study, we integrate transcriptomic and proteomic analyses across independent cohorts to define 111 

reproducible patterns of antibody –drug conjugate (ADC) and immunotherapy target co -expression 112 

in urothelial carcinoma. We identify biologically distinct target -expression patterns that are 113 

associated with histologic and molecular subtypes and demonstrate coordinated and, in some 114 

cases, mutually exclusive relationships among therapeutically actionable targets. 115 

These findings have direct translational implications. First, they provide biologic rationale for 116 

rational sequencing and combination strategies based on co -expressed targets in NECTIN4 -117 

enriched tumors. Second, they identify alternative therapeutic vulnerabilities, including DLL3 - and 118 

CD274 -associated pathways, in tumors with low NECTIN4 expression, a population potentially 119 

enriched for resistance to EV -based therapy. Finally, this framework establishes a foundation for 120 

biomarker -driven clinical trials in urothelial carcinoma and supports the development of precision 121 

therapeutic approaches beyond current standards. 122

Introduction 124 

For decades , first line therapy for locally advanced or metastatic (referred to as advanced hereafter) 125 

urothelial carcinoma (UC) comprised platinum -based chemotherapy but associated with limited 126 

survival benefit and significant adverse effects. Recent studies have demonstrated superior 127 

outcomes with antibody -drug conjugates (ADCs), which selectively deliver cytotoxic payloads to 128 

tumor -associated antigens 1. Together with immune therapies , ADCs have fundamentally 129 

transformed the therapeutic landscape across multiple malignancies by improv ing response rates 130 

and in some cases achieving durable clinical benefit compared with conventional chemotherapy 2,3 .131 

132 

In advanced UC, Nectin -4 targeting ADC , enfortumab vedotin (EV) combined with 133 

pembrolizumab has demonstrated superior overall survival (OS) , achieving a median OS of 31.5 134 

months compared to 16.1 months with chemotherapy 4. This combination (henceforth abbreviated 135 

as EV plus pembrolizumab ) is now the standard first -line treatment for advanced UC 4. Despite 136 

remarkable outcomes , approximately one -third of patients fail to respond to this treatment .137 

Therefore , there is a vital clinical need for treatment strategies for patients who are non -responders 138 

or experience disease progression on EV plus pembrolizumab . ADCs against other molecular 139 

targets are under active investigation for UC as well as other solid tumors and may offer viable 140 

treatment strategies for these patients . Similarly, immunotherapy targets are also advancing to 141 

multiple immune checkpoints and novel bispecific antibodies and may be harnessed for durable 142 

responses. However, the expression landscape and co -occurrence patterns of actionable ADC and 143 

IO targets in relation to Nectin -4 expression remains poorly characterized (or incompletely 144 

defined) in UC .145

Methods 152 

Literature Search 153 

Identification of ADC and IO targets relevant to our study was performed using the following 154 

literature search methodology : Search terms include “bladder cancer ”, “urothelial carcinoma ”,155 

“antibody -drug conjugate ”, “ADC ”, “solid tumor”, and “immunotherapy ”. Molecular targets were 156 

included for downstream analysis based on whether there were therapeutics targeting these 157 

compounds with ongoing clinical trials or had achieved FDA . Targets were not restricted to 158 

urothelial carcinoma but included ADC and IO targets with clinical or investigational relevance 159 

across solid tumors. Molecular targets for hematologic malignancies were excluded. 160

Bioinformatics 176 

Differential gene expression analysis was performed using DESeq2. Unwanted technical variation 177 

was estimated using the RUVSeq package (RUVg, k = 6) with a set of 150 empirically defined 178 

stable genes, and the resulting W factors were incorporated as covariates. RUV -normalized counts 179 

were variance -stabilized using the DESeq2 (v1.48.2) variance stabilizing transformation (VST) 180 

for downstream analyses. RNA -seq differential expression analysis was performed using DESeq2 181 

with a generalized linear model that included CMIBC subtype and covariates (sex, age at specimen 182 

collection, stage, sample type, library preparation method (TruSeq vs. Tagmentation), and W 183 

factors for unwanted variation). Pairwise contrasts between CMIBC subtypes were computed 184 

using Wald tests with adaptive shrinkage of log2 fold changes (ashr), and false discovery rate 185 

(FDR) correction was applied across ADC target genes using the Benjamini –Hochberg method. 186 

Criteria used to determine significance included false discovery rate -adjusted p value less than 187 

0.01 and Log2 fold change with magnitude greater than 1. 188

Discussion 317 

In this study, we define a reproducible and biologically coherent landscape of ADC and 318 

immunotherapy target expression in urothelial carcinoma, identifying three principal patterns of 319 

co -expression that are conserved across independent transcriptomic cohorts and supported by 320 

orthogonal proteomic data. These findings move beyond single -target analyses to establish 321 

coordinated target -expression states , providing a conceptual framework for therapeutic 322 

prioritization in UC. 323 

The clinical relevance of this work is underscored by the evolving treatment paradigm in advanced 324 

UC. Although EV plus pembrolizumab has significantly improved outcomes, a substantial 325 

proportion of patients experience primary resistance or disease progression. In this setting, 326 

treatment selection is not currently guided by tumor biology. Our findings address this gap by 327 

defining distinct target -expression states that may inform rational therapeutic strategies following 328 

EV -based therapy. 329 

A key observation from our analysis is that NECTIN4 expression , central to EV activity , is not 330 

uniformly distributed across tumors and is embedded within a broader network of co -expressed 331 

ADC -associated targets, including FGFR3 , ERBB2 , and TACSTD2 . These findings provide 332 

biologic rationale for combinatorial or sequential ADC approaches targeting co -expressed antigens 333 

in N ectin -4-high tumors . EV plus pembrolizumab has become the first line therapy for patients 334 

with advanced UC and was recently incorporated into treatment paradigms for patients with 335 

cisplatin -ineligible, muscle invasive UC 13 . Given the possibility of achieving a complete response 336 

by adding therapies based on co expression patterns may inform future strategies aimed at 337

presented are illustrative and do not establish efficacy. 374 

Accordingly, our findings require prospective validation in treatment -annotated cohorts. Future 375 

studies incorporating longitudinal sampling and integration of genomic, transcriptomic, and 376 

proteomic data will be essential to define predictive biomarkers and optimize sequencing 377 

strategies. 378
