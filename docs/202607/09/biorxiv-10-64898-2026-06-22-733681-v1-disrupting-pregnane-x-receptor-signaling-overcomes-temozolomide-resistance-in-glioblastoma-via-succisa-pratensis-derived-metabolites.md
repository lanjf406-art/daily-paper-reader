## 摘要
胶质母细胞瘤仍然是一种高度侵袭性且对治疗耐药的脑肿瘤，目前结合手术、放疗和替莫唑胺的标准治疗方案获益有限。因此，克服化疗耐药性代表着一个关键的未满足的临床需求。

在这里，我们研究了断尾草的抗癌潜力及其在胶质母细胞瘤模型中增强替莫唑胺疗效的能力。断尾草处理显著降低了细胞增殖和迁移，同时明显增加了对替莫唑胺的敏感性。综合多组学分析揭示了广泛的代谢重编程，其特征是中心碳代谢的抑制和应激适应途径的激活。

在机制上，我们确定孕烷X受体（PXR），一个药物代谢和化疗耐药性的关键调节因子，是受治疗影响的中心节点。尽管断尾草增加了PXR的表达，但这并未伴随包括MDR1和ALDH1A1在内的典型下游靶标的诱导，表明PXR转录活性的功能受损。一致地，使用拮抗剂SPA70对PXR进行药理抑制进一步增强了断尾草和替莫唑胺的细胞毒作用。

对接分析表明，特定的次级代谢物，包括芹菜素衍生物，可能与PXR配体结合域相互作用，为这一效应提供了潜在的分子基础。

总体而言，我们的发现表明，断尾草通过诱导代谢脆弱性和功能性地损害PXR信号来增强替莫唑胺的疗效。这些结果强调了植物来源的代谢物作为辅助策略以克服胶质母细胞瘤化疗耐药性的治疗潜力。

## Abstract
Glioblastoma remains a highly aggressive and therapy-resistant brain tumor, with limited benefit from the current standard-of-care regimen combining surgery, radiotherapy, and temozolomide. Overcoming chemoresistance therefore represents a critical unmet clinical need.

Here, we investigate the anticancer potential of Succisa pratensis and its ability to enhance TMZ efficacy in GBM models. Treatment with S. pratensis markedly reduced cell proliferation and migration while significantly increasing sensitivity to TMZ. Integrated multi-omics analyses revealed extensive metabolic rewiring, characterized by suppression of central carbon metabolism and activation of stress-adaptive pathways.

Mechanistically, we identify the Pregnane X Receptor, a key regulator of drug metabolism and chemoresistance, as a central node affected by treatment. Although S. pratensis increased PXR expression, this was not accompanied by induction of canonical downstream targets, including MDR1 and ALDH1A1, indicating a functional impairment of PXR transcriptional activity. Consistently, pharmacological inhibition of PXR using the antagonist SPA70 further potentiated the cytotoxic effects of S. pratensis and TMZ.

Docking analyses suggest that specific secondary metabolites, including apigenin-derived compounds, may interact with the PXR ligand-binding domain, providing a potential molecular basis for this effect.

Collectively, our findings indicate that S. pratensis enhances TMZ efficacy by inducing metabolic vulnerability and functionally impairing PXR signaling. These results highlight the therapeutic potential of plant-derived metabolites as adjuvant strategies to overcome chemoresistance in glioblastoma.
