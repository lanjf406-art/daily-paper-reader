## 摘要
肿瘤休眠被认为能够导致癌症复发，但患者中休眠的背景和机制尚不明确。在临床前模型中，已观察到免疫原性肿瘤质量休眠（涉及持续的肿瘤细胞增殖和平衡的免疫介导细胞死亡）以及细胞水平静止。在这里，我们证明了在接受免疫检查点抑制剂（ICI）治疗的黑色素瘤患者的长期稳定残留病变中存在质量休眠而非静止。在一个经过详细空间分析的大型稳定病变中，增殖性肿瘤细胞的比例与ICI进展患者部位匹配的肿瘤相似。其他患者的残留稳定病变，在临床病理学审查中判断为仅含有瘢痕组织，也包含被活跃免疫细胞包围的分裂肿瘤细胞巢。这些发现证明了持久稳定病变中存在活肿瘤细胞和质量休眠，这一发现对疾病监测和管理具有意义。

## Abstract
Tumor dormancy is thought to enable cancer recurrence, but the settings and mechanisms of dormancy in patients are poorly characterized. Both immunogenic tumor mass dormancy, involving continued tumor cell proliferation and balanced immune-mediated cell death, and cell-level quiescence have been observed in preclinical models. Here, we demonstrate the existence of mass dormancy rather than quiescence in long-term stable residual lesions in melanoma patients treated with immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICI). In a large stable lesion subjected to detailed spatial profiling, the proportion of proliferating tumor cells is similar to that in site-matched tumors from patients progressing after ICI. Residual stable lesions from other patients, judged to contain only scar tissue upon clinical pathology review, also contained nests of dividing tumor cells surrounded by active immune cells. These findings demonstrate the presence of viable tumor cells and mass dormancy in persistent stable lesions, a finding with implications for disease monitoring and management.
